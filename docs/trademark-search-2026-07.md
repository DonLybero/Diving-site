## Registration picture

- **No federal (USPTO) registration for "scubanaut(s)" surfaced** in the
  indexed registers searched (Justia, Trademarkia, TrademarkElite) — only
  unrelated "SCUBA…" marks. SCUBAnauts International uses **™** (a claim),
  not **®**, consistent with state + common-law rights only.
- The only registration found is the **Florida state** mark (#2 above) —
  enforceable in Florida, far weaker than a federal ®.
- **Could not verify from this sandbox:** direct USPTO API returned empty
  (proxy), scubanaut.com returned 403 via proxy, and WHOIS/RDAP was blocked —
  so the .com's status and any very recent USPTO filings are unconfirmed.
  Nothing found searching EUIPO/WIPO indexes via the web either, but those
  were not searched natively.

## Risk read (historical — name has since been changed to DiveSZN)

- **The field is crowded, which cuts both ways.** No single global owner
  means nobody has a strong exclusive claim, and many small users coexist —
  but it also means many potential objectors and a **diluted, hard-to-defend
  mark** if Scubanaut-the-site ever wants exclusivity.
- **Direct overlap risk:** the site includes a **Dive Centres directory** —
  the same trade as the Scubanaut-named dive centres in India, Malaysia and
  Greece. Listing or ranking centres under a name identical to theirs is the
  most plausible confusion scenario.
- **Merch is the hottest zone:** scubanautdiving.com already sells
  Scubanaut-branded apparel in the US. Selling Scubanaut merch would collide
  head-on. **Defer merch** until cleared.
- **Florida-specific:** the state mark + the two Florida organisations make
  Florida-targeted marketing the riskiest US geography.
- **SEO reality:** even absent legal issues, "scubanaut" search results are
  shared with 8+ other users — ranking for the bare name will be a fight.

## Recommendations

1. **Attorney knockout search** (USPTO + EUIPO + WIPO + common-law) before
   any merch or paid-ad spend. (~US$300–600 typically.)
2. If cleared, **file in the actual service class** — online travel /
   destination-information services (Nice classes 39/41/42), not dive
   instruction — in the home jurisdiction first. The niche appears open.
3. **Keep the visual identity distinct** (bare whale-fluke mark, deep-teal
   instrument-panel design) — none of the existing users look like this, and
   distinct trade dress is the best practical insulation.
4. **Document first use now:** repo history, deploy dates, an archive.org
   snapshot of the live site.
5. **No Scubanaut-branded merchandise** until step 1 clears it.
6. Consider a distinguishing lockup for marketing ("Scubanaut — the dive
   planner") and check alternative domains, since scubanaut.com appears
   taken (unverified).
