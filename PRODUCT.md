# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

Scuba divers planning their next trip. They arrive with a constraint — dates they can travel, or a destination they're curious about — and the job to be done is confidence: knowing before they book that the water will be worth the flight. A secondary mode of the same person browses gear guides between trips. They are hobbyists to serious amateurs, not professionals; they read English, plan in months and half-months, and compare options across the whole world rather than one region.

## Product Purpose

DiveSZN is a seasonal dive-trip planner and dive hub: 83 destinations scored across all twelve months (996 destination-months, 620 documented dive sites), wrapped in researched gear buyer's guides, monthly destination articles and marine-life encounter pages. It exists so a diver never books a trip that lands in the wrong season. Success is affiliate revenue — booking and gear commissions earned because the research was good enough to act on.

## Positioning

The most researched dive site on the internet. Every screen reinforces one claim: this data is real, checked, and complete — so you can act on it.

## Conversion & proof

- Primary CTA: book a stay via the booking links attached to destinations ("Stays near …"). Secondary: gear buy links through authorised dealers in the gear guide.
- The line a visitor remembers after 10 seconds: "The most researched dive site on the internet."
- Belief ladder: (1) this data is real research, not filler — the calendar, temps, visibility and named dive sites prove someone did the work; (2) this destination at this month is right for *my* dates; (3) booking the stay through the page I'm already on is the natural next step.
- Proof on hand: the data itself — 996 scored destination-months, 620 named dive sites with depth/level/character, real attributed photography, a published scoring method (how-we-score page). No testimonials or press; the research is the proof.

## Brand Personality

A confident, research-driven dive publication: light, precise, a little nautical. Three words: researched, calm, exact. The interface should evoke the feeling of a well-kept logbook — quiet confidence backed by numbers, never hype. The sell stays quiet: coral appears only on buy/booking actions, prices sit in ink-colored monospace, and the homepage carries no prices at all.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated travel templates: aphorism intros, "hidden gem / paradise / bucket list / breathtaking" vocabulary, stock-photo gloss.
- Snorkelling/freediving lifestyle content — this is a scuba publication (the only exception is explicitly labelled "beyond scuba" marine-life sections).
- Third-party name-dropping as authority (certification agencies, magazines, testers) — the site's own research is the authority.
- Coupon-site energy: coral-everywhere pricing, urgency banners, countdown timers.
- Dive-cliché branding: helmets, bubbles-in-circles, generic wave logos.

## Design Principles

1. **Research is the product.** Every published number, site and photo is real and checked; if the data is thin, write shorter — never invent. Design choices should make the depth of research visible (data readouts, season calendars, evidence ledgers).
2. **Quiet commerce.** Monetization never outshouts the data. Coral is reserved for buy/booking CTAs; everything else earns trust first.
3. **Data has a voice.** Monospace readouts, the tonal season palette, and the 12-month calendar are the brand's signature devices — prefer them over new chart types.
4. **One orchestrated moment per view.** Motion is choreographed and purposeful; restraint everywhere else. Reduced motion is always honored.
5. **Concrete or silent.** Copy is grounded in the destination's own data — named sites, real temps, actual species. No filler, ever.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG commitment declared, but practiced standards: prefers-reduced-motion alternatives for all animation, keyboard operability for interactive elements (region picker, calendar, planner), aria-live announcements for dynamic results, white text only on the darkest season tone, and tap targets ≥40px on mobile.
