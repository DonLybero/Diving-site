# Indonesia dive trip: one country, two ultimate trips

Here is the honest finding up front: you cannot plan one Indonesia dive trip that catches everything at its best. Bali, Nusa Penida, the Gilis and Komodo all peak in the northern summer — every one of them is at Peak in July. Raja Ampat is the mirror image: its season runs December to March, and in July most of its boats have left for calmer water. Any route that chains all five quietly sacrifices somebody. So this plan doesn't. It gives you two trips, each with every stop at its best, and for each stop the number of days it actually takes to see the key things — justified against the dive-site list and the topside list, not guessed.

## When each destination is at its best

| Destination | Peak months | Good months |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bali | Apr–Jul, Oct–Nov | Mar, Aug–Sep |
| Nusa Penida | Jul–Sep | Apr–Jun, Oct |
| Gili Islands & Lombok | May–Sep | Mar–Apr, Oct |
| Komodo | Apr–Oct | rest of year |
| Raja Ampat | Dec–Mar | Apr, Oct–Nov |

Read the table twice and the two trips fall out on their own. The top four rows all contain July; the bottom row never overlaps a Peak month with any of them. The closest Raja Ampat comes to the rest is October to April at Good — respectable, never both at Peak.

## Trip one — the dry-season route (July, at absolute peak)

**Bali 5 days → Nusa Penida 3 → Gili Islands 3 → Komodo 5. Sixteen days on the ground, about 18 with transfers.**

In July all four stops sit at Peak simultaneously — the only month that's true. It's also the opening of mola-mola season on Nusa Penida, when cold upwellings pull sunfish onto the Crystal Bay cleaning stations, and mid dry season in Komodo, with mantas stacked over Makassar Reef and calm seas for the northern pinnacles. The route runs west to east on one flight corridor: land at Denpasar, fast boat to Penida, fast boat onward to the Gilis, boat back to Bali and a one-hour flight to Labuan Bajo, fly home from there.

Can't travel in July? The whole route holds at Good-or-better from April to October, so any month in that window works — you're trading Peak for Good on one or two legs, and the table above shows exactly which.

## Trip two — Raja Ampat (December to March)

**Nine days door to door from Jakarta, seven of them diving.**

Raja Ampat is its own trip because the geography demands it and the season confirms it. Getting in costs most of a day each way — a roughly 4 h flight to Sorong, usually a red-eye from Jakarta, then the ~2 h ferry to Waisai and a boat pickup — and the consensus on dive time matches that overhead: liveaboards there start at 7 nights and commonly run 10–12, and the standard homestay dive packages in the Dampier Strait run 6–7 nights. Under about seven dive days, the transit-to-diving ratio stops making sense. December to March is the heart of the season: flat seas, 25–40 m visibility, mantas thick at the cleaning stations.

One legitimate add-on: Komodo sits at Good from January to March, so a Raja Ampat trip can bolt on a Komodo leg via Jakarta or Makassar. Be clear-eyed about the label — that's Komodo in good, not peak, condition, with wetter weather and fewer boats than its April–October prime.

## The ultimate 5 days in Bali

Five days because the diving splits into three natural boat-and-shore days, the topside list is the longest in Indonesia, and you land here jet-lagged anyway. Dive guides converge on 3–5 days for the northeast coast; five lets you add a west-coast day and a real surface day.

Day one is arrival and Sanur. Days two and three belong to the Tulamben–Amed shore-entry coast: the USAT Liberty — a 120 m WWII cargo ship 30 m off the beach in 5–30 m of water, with bumphead parrotfish grazing it at dawn — plus the Tulamben Drop-Off's gorgonian wall, the black-sand macro slope at Seraya Secrets (harlequin shrimp, ghost pipefish) and the Jemeluk pyramids at Amed. Day four goes either to Blue Lagoon at Padang Bai, where rhinopias and blue-ringed octopus turn up in a calm white-sand bay, or across to Menjangan Island's gorgonian-draped walls in the island's clearest water. Day five is the topside day and your pre-flight no-dive buffer: the Mount Batur sunrise trek puts you on a 1,717 m crater rim by dawn, and the kecak dance at Uluwatu ends the trip with a 60-man chanting choir on a clifftop 70 m above the surf.

Base in Amed or Tulamben for the dive days, then finish in Sanur — a calm beachfront grid with the Penida fast-boat harbour at its doorstep. Getting in: fly to Denpasar (DPS), nonstop from most major Asian and Australian hubs plus Doha and Istanbul.

## The ultimate 3 days in Nusa Penida

Three days because mola-mola are never guaranteed and the working advice from Penida operators is 2–3 dive days to give yourself real odds — six to nine dives instead of a day-tripper's two. The island's seven key sites fit that exactly: two mornings at Crystal Bay, the classic sunfish site where July–October upwellings drop the water to 24–25 °C, with afternoons at Manta Point or the calmer Manta Bay, where reef mantas hold cleaning stations year-round. The third day covers the channel — the Toyapakeh wall, the SD Point high-speed drift, or macro in sheltered Gamat Bay. This is the advanced leg: the Indonesian Throughflow funnels between the islands, driving fast drifts and sudden down-currents that demand solid buoyancy and guide-led planning.

Late afternoons absorb the limestone topside in half-day bites: the Kelingking headland, the collapsed sea-cave arch at Broken Beach, or the Peguyangan stairs — a steel staircase bolted down a 200 m sea cliff to a spring-fed temple. Stay on the Ped–Sampalan north-coast strip near the dive shops. Fast boats run from Sanur to Toyapakeh or Sampalan about 21 times daily (30–45 min, IDR 150,000–250,000).

## The ultimate 3 days in the Gili Islands

Three days because the standing advice for divers here is two to four, and the eight key sites sort into three two-dive days with no repetition: Turtle Heaven, a pinnacle north of Gili Meno stacked with green and hawksbill turtles, paired with Shark Point's reef sharks and rays; Halik Reef's drift canyons or the deep seamounts of Deep Turbo; then Han's Reef off Gili Air for critters — frogfish, seahorses, ribbon eels — with Meno Wall as the area's best night dive. After two current-heavy legs, the mild conditions are the point, not a compromise.

The three cays are flat, car-free and easily filled between dives: cycle Trawangan's 7 km coastal loop in about an hour, eat grilled reef fish at the harbour night market before the best of it sells out around 20:00, or hop the public boats to Meno's salt lake. Gili Trawangan has the widest spread of restaurants and dive shops; Gili Air is the quieter middle ground. Direct fast boats run from Padang Bai or Serangan (1.5–2.5 h, ~USD 22–35); for Komodo, boat back to Bali and fly from Denpasar.

## The ultimate 5 days in Komodo

Five days because the park's nine key sites are current-timed, and the liveaboard market has already done the maths: most itineraries run 5–7 nights with four to six diving days, and a four-dive-day trip covers the central and northern park plus the topside icons. Tidal flow peaks at full and new moon, and the best sites are built on it — Batu Bolong, a current-swept pinnacle dense with fish; Castle Rock and Crystal Rock, seamounts where sharks and trevally hunt in the flow; The Cauldron, a channel dive fired through a bowl by ripping current. Manta Point (Makassar Reef) is a long shallow drift past cleaning stations at 6–15 m; calmer days go to Siaba Besar, the sheltered bay dubbed Turtle City. Four dive days at three dives a day works through all of it with slack-window timing to spare.

The fifth day is the park's topside set, usually as one boat day: the stair climb to Padar Island's three-bay ridge view, a ranger-guided dragon walk on Komodo or Rinca — the only place on earth to see wild Komodo dragons — and the pink sand of Pantai Merah. A liveaboard folds it all together; land-based works too, from the Labuan Bajo harbourfront, steps from the jetties for 05:30 departures. Fly Denpasar to Labuan Bajo (LBJ), about 1 h nonstop, 4–6 daily flights.

## The ultimate 9 days in Raja Ampat

Nine days: seven diving, plus a travel day at each end that the geography makes non-negotiable. Seven dive days is where the recommendations converge — 7 nights is the floor for liveaboards, 6–7 nights the standard Dampier Strait homestay package — and it's what the site list needs. Cape Kri holds a world-record fish count for a single dive; Blue Magic is a seamount visited by oceanic mantas and wobbegongs; Manta Sandy is a shallow cleaning station with a marked viewing line; Sardine Reef is one of the fishiest sites in the strait; Melissa's Garden is a pristine hard-coral plateau with pygmy seahorses; The Passage runs like a river between Waigeo and Gam; Arborek Jetty covers macro. Seven days at two to three dives a day covers that list and leaves room to repeat a favourite.

The non-diving hours fill themselves: a full boat day to the 320-step Piaynemo boardwalk over the cone-karst lagoon, a 04:30 walk to watch red birds-of-paradise display at Saporkren, a paddle through Kabui Bay's mangroves. Stay on Kri or Mansuar, minutes by boat from the busiest reefs, or quieter Gam. Life runs on boat schedules, rainwater tanks and generator hours — bring cash and dry bags; Waisai has the ATMs and permit offices, so withdraw everything before the boat pickup.

## Events worth timing — or planning around

- **Nyepi (19 March in 2026).** Bali's Day of Silence stops the province for 24 h — Denpasar airport closes, roads empty, Penida's boats stop. It sits inside Raja Ampat trip season, so mind it if you're routing home through Bali.
- **Galungan & Kuningan (17 and 27 June in 2026).** Penjor poles arch over every Balinese street; Penida's Pura Dalem Ped fills with pilgrims and fast boats sell out around the holy days — just before the July window.
- **Raja Ampat Festival (mid-to-late October, Waisai).** Ferries and town rooms run full; book the Sorong–Waisai crossing early if a shoulder-season trip lands on it.
- **Lebaran (March in 2026).** A week-long national travel surge — domestic flights and crossings fill hardest, and it lands mid Raja Ampat season.

## The practical bits

- **No-fly days.** Build a full no-dive day before every flight. On trip one that's before the Denpasar–Labuan Bajo hop and before flying home; the topside days above absorb them. On trip two, the ferry-plus-red-eye exit does the same job.
- **Permits.** Raja Ampat requires a marine-park permit (IDR 700,000) plus a visitor entry ticket (IDR 300,000), payable in Waisai or online. Komodo diving happens inside a national park, so park fees apply on every boat day — confirm current rates when booking.
- **Domestic legs.** Trip one needs one flight: DPS–LBJ, 1 h, 4–6 daily. Trip two runs Jakarta–Sorong (~4 h, usually overnight) and back; the Komodo add-on connects via Jakarta or Makassar. Book the LBJ and SOQ legs first — they have the least capacity.
