# Red Sea dive trip: four Egyptian bases, one month when they all peak

Most multi-stop dive trips force a compromise: one destination is firing, the next is merely open. Egypt is the rare exception. Its four Red Sea bases — Sharm El Sheikh and Dahab on the Sinai side, Hurghada & El Gouna and Marsa Alam on the mainland — all hit peak season in the same month, and that month is September. Late-summer water at 28°C, calm crossings to the outer reefs, snapper still massed at Ras Mohammed and the first oceanic whitetips arriving at Elphinstone. If you have ever wanted to link drift walls, walk-in shore dives, a wreck graveyard and big-animal reefs in one Red Sea dive trip without diving anything out of season, this is the country — and September is the window.

## When each base is at its best

The route logic below follows one rule: only combine bases that are great at the same time. Here is how the four seasons stack up.

| Base | Peak | Good |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sharm El Sheikh | Jun-Sep | Apr-May, Oct-Nov |
| Dahab | Jun, Sep-Oct | Apr-May, Jul-Aug, Nov |
| Hurghada & El Gouna | Apr-Jun, Sep-Nov | Mar, Jul-Aug |
| Marsa Alam | Sep-Nov | Mar-Aug, Dec |

Read it two ways. All four bases sit at peak simultaneously in exactly one month: September. And all four are at least good from April through November, so a shoulder-season trip still works — you just won't have every stop at its best. Winter brings northerly wind, chop and cancelled outer-reef runs; July-August trades flat seas for hot decks and crowds.

## The full route — all four bases in September (15-16 days)

Do Sinai first, then the mainland, or reverse it — the seasons don't care, so let flight prices decide. Crossing between the two sides needs a flight via Cairo (or a very long day of road transfers), so treat it as a full travel day. That is a feature, not a cost: the crossing day doubles as the no-fly buffer after your last Sinai dive.

The stops, using each base's researched ideal:

- **Sharm El Sheikh — 4 days.** Three boat runs (Tiran, Ras Mohammed, the SS Thistlegorm) plus the Mount Sinai night climb.
- **Dahab — 3 days.** The standalone ideal is 4, but the fourth day exists mainly as a dry buffer before flying — and here the Cairo crossing absorbs it. Three self-paced shore days at up to 3 dives each cover the town's headline sites.
- **Crossing day — 1.** Road back to Sharm El Sheikh (1.5 h), fly to Hurghada via Cairo. No-fly buffer done.
- **Hurghada & El Gouna — 4 days.** Three boat days for the wrecks and offshore pinnacles, one for Luxor.
- **Marsa Alam — 3-4 days.** Two chances at an Elphinstone weather window, the turtle bays, and a Wadi el Gemal day that doubles as the final no-fly buffer.

Total: 15-16 days, every stop in peak season, and both mandatory dry days spent on Mount Sinai's timetable and Egypt's desert rather than a sunbed.

## One week only? The two season-matched pairs

If you can't stretch to two weeks, the four bases split into two pairs that are linked by road and matched by season.

**The Sinai pair: Sharm El Sheikh + Dahab, 7-8 days, in June or September.** Those are the two months both bases are at peak together. Fly into Sharm El Sheikh (SSH), spend 4 days there, then transfer 90 km north to Dahab — about 1.5 hours by road, roughly USD 25-45 pre-booked — for 3-4 days of shore diving before the drive back.

**The mainland pair: Hurghada & El Gouna + Marsa Alam, 8 days, September to November.** The pair peaks together for three full months, and the back half of that window overlaps Elphinstone's oceanic-whitetip season, which runs October into December. Fly into Hurghada (HRG), 4 days there, then 215 km south — 3.5-4 hours by road — for 4 days on the Marsa Alam coast, flying home from Marsa Alam (RMF) if routings allow.

## The ultimate 4 days in Sharm El Sheikh

Four days because Sharm's diving divides into three distinct boat runs, and the topside headliner eats a night. Day 1: the Straits of Tiran, where Jackson Reef hangs scalloped hammerheads off its exposed north face in summer and Thomas Reef rings its deep canyon with soft corals and gorgonians. Day 2: Ras Mohammed — Shark & Yolanda Reef's twin pinnacles above the scattered toilet-bowl cargo of the Yolanda, where bohar snapper and barracuda mass in spawning aggregations June to September, then the sandy drift of Jackfish Alley past resting whitetips. Day 3: the early-start run to the SS Thistlegorm, the WWII supply ship whose holds still carry motorbikes, trucks and rifles at 16-32 m. Day 4 goes topside: a night ascent of Mount Sinai to 2,285 m for sunrise (the monastery below closes to visitors on Sundays), or Ras Mohammed by jeep and the Old Market souk with grilled fish priced by the kilo. Ras Um Sid's gorgonian forest works from shore in most weather if you want a fourth diving morning instead.

Stay in Naama Bay for the central promenade, Hadaba/Ras Um Sid for clifftop quiet and house reefs off the rocks, or Sharks Bay for quick access to Tiran boats. SSH takes direct flights from much of Europe and the Gulf plus roughly hourly Cairo connections; the airport is 15-25 minutes from Naama Bay.

## The ultimate 4 days in Dahab

Four days standalone — three if the trip continues elsewhere. Dahab has no boat schedule: nearly everything is a walk-in shore dive on the near-currentless Gulf of Aqaba, so three dives a day is realistic without early alarms. Day 1: the Blue Hole — down the Bells chimney, along the outer wall and over the 7 m saddle into the sinkhole — then the Canyon, a crack in the reef plate opening into a fishbowl cavern. Day 2: the town reefs — Lighthouse Reef with its resident frogfish and seahorses (also the go-to night dive), Eel Garden's swaying colony, and the hard-coral maze of the Islands. Day 3: the camel-or-boat trek to Gabr el Bint's pristine gorgonian wall, or repeat favourites. Day 4 stays dry: the jeep-and-camel run past the Blue Hole to the Blue Lagoon at Ras Abu Galum for a fish lunch in a palm hut, or simply tea on the carpeted cafe terraces ringing the Blue Hole's rim. Visibility peaks around 30 m in late summer.

Stay in Masbat for the seafront promenade, Mashraba for the quieter end of the same strip, or Assalah for the cheapest long-stay rooms. Fly to SSH and transfer 90 km over the mountains, about 1 hour 15 minutes to 1.5 hours.

## The ultimate 4 days in Hurghada & El Gouna

Four days because the diving fills three boat days and the region holds Egypt's only realistic Luxor day trip. Day 1: Abu Nuhas, the reef that collects ships — the Giannis D with its glassfish-filled engine room, the 1869 Carnatic whose exposed ribs are sheathed in soft coral, and the Chrisoula K still stacked with Italian floor tiles. Day 2: the offshore pinnacles — Careless Reef, the area's most reliable spot for grey reef sharks an hour out, and Umm Gamar's cavern swim-through at 28 m. Day 3: the Small Giftun drift wall and Shaab El Erg's lagoon, a daytime rest stop for resident spinner dolphins. Day 4: Luxor — 280 km and 4 hours each way for Karnak and the Valley of the Kings, a 14-16 hour day that conveniently keeps you out of the water. Softer swaps: a Giftun Island beach day or the El Dahar souk and fish market.

Stay in El Gouna (Downtown or Abu Tig Marina), Hurghada Marina/Sekalla near the dive jetties, or El Dahar for budget rooms. HRG takes direct flights from most of Europe; El Gouna is a 25 km, 30-45 minute transfer north.

## The ultimate 4 days in Marsa Alam

Four days because Elphinstone runs to a weather window and deserves two chances at it. Days 1 and 2: hold both for the open-sea finger reef whose north and south plateaus draw patrolling oceanic whitetips from October to December, in currents that demand experience — dive it the first calm day, and spend the other at Sha'ab Samadai's dolphin lagoon and caverns. Day 3: the easy south — Abu Dabbab's walk-in seagrass bay where green turtles graze metres from shore (dugong turn up rarely), and Marsa Shagra's house reef with twin entry channels divable on your own schedule. Day 4 stays dry as the no-fly buffer: Wadi el Gemal National Park's jeep-and-camel routes past Roman-era emerald mine settlements, or El Quseir's 16th-century fort an hour north. One honest caveat: Daedalus Reef, the schooling-hammerhead pinnacle, is liveaboard range — the standard safaris out of Port Ghalib run 7 nights, so treat that as a separate trip, not a day.

Stay at Port Ghalib for the marina, the Abu Dabbab bays for turtles off the beach, or Hamata in the deep south for reefs and quiet. RMF is 10 minutes from Port Ghalib but 45-90 minutes from most south-coast resorts; Hurghada is the road fallback if you can't route into RMF.

## Events and timing

Ramadan moves about 11 days earlier each year (18 February-19 March in 2026); resorts and boats run normally, but towns go quiet by day and alcohol service can be limited outside hotels. The two Eid holiday weeks fill Red Sea rooms with domestic travellers — book ahead or shift dates. The El Gouna Film Festival takes over that town for nine days in mid-to-late October, so a mainland trip then should base in Hurghada. Christmas through the first week of January means peak pricing plus compulsory gala-dinner supplements on 31 December. None of these touch September.

## The practical bits

Leave 18-24 hours between your last dive and any flight — the Cairo crossing and the Wadi el Gemal day are built into the route for exactly this. Transfer ballparks: SSH to Naama Bay USD 8-15, SSH to Dahab USD 25-45 pre-booked, HRG to El Gouna EUR 17-25, RMF to the southern resorts EUR 25-50. Airport-rank taxis expect a negotiated price — agree the fare before getting in. Self-drive isn't worth it anywhere here; pre-booked cars and shuttles cover everything. Water runs 28-29°C in September — a 3 mm suit is plenty.
