Bay Islands

Best Feb–Apr & Oct–Dec27–30°CViz to 32 m

Strung along the Mesoamerican Reef off Honduras, Roatan, Utila and Guanaja deliver some of the Caribbean's most affordable wall diving, with seasonal whale sharks as the headline pelagic. Guanaja adds volcanic canyons, a black-coral seamount and the intact Jado Trader wreck at 24-33 m, while the Cayos Cochinos marine reserve keeps pristine elkhorn shallows. Currents stay mostly mild, and shore entries, day boats and liveaboards all work here.

Bay Islands is at its best Feb-Apr & Oct-Dec (whale sharks), with Feb, Mar, Apr and May rating as peak season. Expect tropical water of 27–30°C (suit: 3mm), visibility up to ~32m, and low currents — Sheltered fringing reefs, light currents. Divers here work 9 recognised sites — 3 walls, 2 wrecks, 2 reefs, 1 cavern and 1 pinnacle — ranging from Mestizo Reef (8-20m) for beginner divers up to The Pinnacle (17-41m), advanced territory.

What to expect down there

Expect classic Caribbean wall diving: you drop over sheer faces like Vertigo and follow drop-offs that simply end in blue. At Jim's Silverlode a tunnel at 21 m spits you into a sandy amphitheatre ringed by grouper, Black Rock Canyon threads volcanic caves lit by silversides and sweepers, and The Pinnacle climbs from 41 m to a black-coral summit at 17 m. Water sits at 27-30°C in a 3 mm, currents stay light, and spring visibility can top 30 m.

What you’ll encounter

Whale sharks are the headline, feeding off Utila from late winter into spring and reappearing offshore from October through December. Between sightings the reef carries the day: turtles graze the sponge-covered walls year-round, eagle rays sail past the drop-offs, nurse sharks doze under ledges, and seahorses cling to the inshore seagrass. Tarpon hang in the caverns at Pelican Point in the Cayos Cochinos reserve.
