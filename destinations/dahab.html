Dahab

Egypt · Red Sea / Egypt — Walk-in Gulf of Aqaba diving at its most laid-back — the Blue Hole via El Bells, the Canyon's cathedral cracks, Lighthouse house-reef ease and a deep technical scene, all from the beach.

Photo: No machine-readable author provided. Nowic assumed (based on copyright claims). (Public domain) via Wikimedia Commons

Dahab is the Red Sea at its most relaxed: a small Sinai town on the Gulf of Aqaba where nearly everything is dived from the beach. Gear up, wade in, and you're on the Canyon's glowing cavern, Lighthouse's critter-rich house reef or the Blue Hole via the Bells. Water stays calm and clear most of the year, winters run cooler, and a serious technical community pushes the deep lines. Ideal for low-stress repetitive diving, training and long stays.

Dahab is at its best Apr-Jun & Sep-Nov, with Jun, Sep and Oct rating as peak season. Expect tropical water of 21–28°C (suit: 3mm in high summer (Jul-Sep)), visibility up to ~30m, and low currents — calm, near-currentless shore diving most days. Divers here work 7 recognised sites — 3 reefs, 2 walls, 1 cave and 1 cavern — ranging from Lighthouse Reef (5-25m) for beginner divers up to The Arch (Blue Hole) (55-60m), technical-diving territory.

Currents detailThe Gulf of Aqaba is sheltered and tide-weak, so most Dahab sites sit in still or gently drifting water. Wind can add surface chop and a mild longshore push at exposed entries like the Blue Hole, but true current is rare.
What to pack here

Water sits around 25°C here, so a 3 mm wetsuit is the right call. A 7 mm is worth packing for winter or deeper, repeat dives.
