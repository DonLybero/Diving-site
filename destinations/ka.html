Kaş

Turkey · Mediterranean / Turkey — The premier dive base on Turkey's Turquoise Coast, with 30-plus sites minutes from the harbour: dramatic walls and the Canyon, the intact sunken DC-3 Dakota, a full-size replica of the Bronze Age Uluburun wreck, amphora fields and swim-through caverns, all in famously clear warm-summer water.

Photo: Tim Rademacher (CC BY-SA 4.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Kaş is at its best ['Jun', 'Jul', 'Aug', 'Sep', 'Oct'], with Jun, Jul, Aug and Sep rating as peak season. Expect temperate/warm Mediterranean sea water of 16–28°C (suit: 5mm), visibility up to ~35m, and mild to moderate currents — Most sheltered reefs are calm, but exposed sites like the Canyon can run a brisk southerly current across the shallow gorge before the wall. Divers here work 7 recognised sites — 2 wrecks, 1 wall / canyon, 1 reef / wall, 1 wreck / archaeological, 1 archaeological / reef and 1 cavern / wall — ranging from Uluburun Replica (Kaş Archaeopark) (8-22m) for beginner divers up to Fener (Lighthouse) Cave (5-30m), advanced territory.

What to pack here

Water sits around 21°C here, so a 3 mm wetsuit is the right call. A 5 mm is worth packing for winter or deeper, repeat dives.

Recognised dive sites (7)

Depths are typical published ranges — always confirm with your operator.

SiteTypeDepthLevelWhy it’s known
The Canyonwall / canyon18-30mintermediateKaş signature dive: cross a shallow gorge, often against a southerly current, into a rift between two steep walls dropping past 20m, cloaked in yellow cluster anemones and sponges with a small cave and red shrimp.
Big Reefreef / wall10-35mintermediateA classic sloping reef and wall system with terraces, cracks and overhangs; grouper, moray, octopus and passing amberjack against superb Turquoise-Coast visibility.
DC-3 Dakota Plane Wreckwreck18-25mintermediateA WWII-era Dakota transport plane deliberately sunk as an artificial reef; the intact fuselage sits on sand at around 25m, now colonised by fish, morays and encrusting growth, with a swim-through interior for trained wreck divers.
Uluburun Replica (Kaş Archaeopark)wreck / archaeological8-22mbeginnerA full-size replica of the Late Bronze Age Uluburun ship laid out in Hidayet Bay with copper ingots and amphorae, an accessible museum-style dive celebrating the region's underwater archaeology.
Flying Fish (Dimitri) Wreckwreck12-28mintermediateA small coastal wreck on a rocky slope that has become a fish magnet, with amberjack, barracuda and grouper patrolling the structure and morays in the recesses.
Amphora Fieldarchaeological / reef10-20mbeginnerA scatter of ancient clay amphorae across a gentle reef, evidence of the coast's trading past, with octopus and moray sheltering inside the vessels.
Fener (Lighthouse) Cavecavern / wall5-30madvancedA dramatic wall pierced by caverns and swim-throughs near the lighthouse point, with shafts of light, lobster and shrimp in the dark recesses and grouper along the drop-off.
