Medes Islands

Spain · Mediterranean — Costa Brava marine reserve famed for huge friendly groupers

Photo: Julien Renoult (CC BY 4.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Seven limestone islets off Spain's Costa Brava, the Medes Islands show what decades of strict protection do for the Mediterranean: outsized, fearless dusky groupers that swim out to inspect divers, clouds of barracuda, and gorgonian-draped walls falling toward 50 m. Tunnels like El Dofi thread clean through the rock with theatrical light shafts. Boat rides from L'Estartit take minutes, currents stay mild, and the sites layer neatly from easy caverns to deep pinnacle dives.

Medes Islands is at its best Jun-Oct, with Jun, Jul, Aug and Sep rating as peak season. Expect saltwater of 13–25°C (suit: 5mm summer, 7mm shoulder season), visibility up to ~20m, and low currents — Protected reserve, mild except pinnacles. Divers here work 9 recognised sites — 3 pinnacles, 2 reefs, 1 cavern, 1 cave, 1 wall and 1 wreck — ranging from Salpatxot (5-24m) for beginner divers up to Reggio Messina (27-35m), advanced territory.

What to expect down there

Limestone does the work here. El Dofi's tunnel runs 90 m clean through the rock with theatrical light at both ends, La Vaca pierces Meda Gran from an entrance at 12 m, and Carall Bernat drops gorgonian-covered walls from 5 m toward 45 m. Currents stay mild inside the reserve, visibility sits around 15-20 m in season, and summer water reaches 21-25°C, so a 5mm suit keeps it all comfortable.

What you’ll encounter

Dusky groupers are the show: decades of protection have made them huge and curious, and they will swim out to meet you on almost any wall. Barracuda school over the pinnacles through summer, moray eels and octopus work the boulders, red coral colours the shaded walls, and in high summer there is an outside chance of a sunfish at the deeper sites.

The honest picture

What can change on the day?

  • Seasons drift. Marine-life timing shifts year to year with plankton blooms and lunar cycles — a peak landing a couple of weeks early or late is normal, not bad luck.
  • Temperatures are ranges. Water figures here are typical monthly ranges (±1°C); an upwelling or a heatwave can step outside them.
  • Visibility is seasonal, not daily. Wind, swell, rain and plankton move it day to day — read our metres as the month's typical form, not a promise for your dive.
  • Currents can run above the rating. Conditions on the day decide — brief with your operator before the first dive, and sit one out if it's beyond your training.
  • A score compares, it doesn't guarantee. We rank months from dive-operator and liveaboard calendars so you can weigh destinations like for like — always confirm current conditions with a local dive centre.
