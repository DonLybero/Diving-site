Fuvahmulah

Best Dec–Apr28–30°CViz to 35 m

A lone oceanic island in the Maldives' far south, Fuvahmulah has no sheltering atoll, just deep water on every side and the most reliable tiger shark diving on Earth. Named resident tigers patrol the shallow harbor plateau at arm's length, while drifts along the drop-offs deliver thresher shark cleaning stations, schooling hammerheads and oceanic mantas. Day boats run everything, and the exposed blue-water diving favors confident, current-comfortable divers.

Fuvahmulah is at its best Dec-Apr (calm dry-season seas and best visibility; tiger sharks year-round), with Jan, Feb, Mar, Apr and Dec rating as peak season. Expect tropical water of 28–30°C (suit: 3mm), visibility up to ~35m, and strong currents — open blue-water drop-offs, tide-driven. Divers here work 7 recognised sites — 1 shark plateau, 1 shark reef, 1 deep reef / cleaning station, 1 reef / big animal, 1 wall / drop-off, 1 reef / drift and 1 deep reef — ranging from Tiger Harbour (5-12m) for intermediate divers up to Razwa Faru (15-40m), advanced territory.

What to expect down there

There is no lagoon to hide in — the reef falls straight into oceanic depth on every side, so most dives run as live drifts along walls and drop-offs with the blue at your shoulder. The counterpoint is the harbour plateau: 5-12 m of clear water over rock where tiger sharks pass close enough to fill your mask. The water sits at 28-30°C year-round; visibility reaches 35 m in the northeast monsoon and drops nearer 25 m in the wet southwest season.

What you’ll encounter

Resident tiger sharks show daily in every month — that is the guarantee. Around them the calendar rotates: thresher sharks rise to the deep cleaning stations most reliably from October into December, scalloped hammerheads school through the dry northeast monsoon, and oceanic mantas gather to clean, feed and mate around April and May. Silvertips can mass in the hundreds in February, with whale sharks passing through the dry months.
