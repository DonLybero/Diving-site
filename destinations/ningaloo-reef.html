Ningaloo Reef

Australia · Indian Ocean / Western Australia — Whale shark snorkeling on the reef plus year-round mantas and turtles

Photo: NeilsPhotography (CC BY 2.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Australia's largest fringing reef hugs the Western Australian coast, its coral gardens starting a short swim off the beach. The diving runs mellow and animal-rich: manta rays circling the cleaning station at Gulliver's, reef sharks queuing over cabbage coral at Asho's Gap, green turtles napping in limestone swim-throughs. The Exmouth Navy Pier alone justifies the trip, its pylons crowded with wobbegongs and frogfish, and whale sharks gather along the reef from March to July.

Ningaloo Reef is at its best Mar-Jul (whale sharks), with Apr, May, Jun and Jul rating as peak season. Expect tropical/subtropical water of 22–29°C (suit: 3mm, 5mm winter), visibility up to ~32m, and low currents — Lagoon gentle; tidal passes need slack-tide planning. Divers here work 7 recognised sites — 6 reefs and 1 shore dive — ranging from Blizzard Ridge (8-16m) for beginner divers up to Helga's Tunnels (8-16m), intermediate territory.

What to expect down there

Most of Ningaloo's diving sits shallow, 6 to 18 m over limestone craters, tunnels and cabbage-coral plateaus, with a gentle lagoon and only the tidal passes needing slack-water timing. You weave The Labyrinth's swim-throughs past napping green turtles, hang at Gulliver's cleaning station as mantas circle in, and pick frogfish and wobbegongs off the pylons of the Exmouth Navy Pier. Water runs 22°C in the austral winter to 29°C in summer, with visibility past 30 m on the calmest days.

What you’ll encounter

Whale sharks headline from March to July, drawn in by the coral spawn, and as their season fades humpback whales stream past the reef into October. Mantas and green turtles are the year-round constants, reef sharks queue over the cabbage coral at Asho's Gap, and the Muiron Islands add rays and the occasional dugong. Turtles nest on the beaches through the summer months.
