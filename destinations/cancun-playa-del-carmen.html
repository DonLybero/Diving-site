Cancún & Playa del Carmen

Mexico · Caribbean / Mexico — Two headline shark seasons — cageless bull shark dives at 24m off Playa del Carmen from November to March and the planet's biggest whale shark aggregation offshore in July-August — bolted onto easy drift reefs, hurricane-split wrecks and the sculpture galleries of the MUSA underwater museum on Manchones reef.

Photo: Safa in la but uploaded by Mardetanha (CC BY-SA 3.0) via Wikimedia Commons

The Cancún–Playa del Carmen corridor is high-volume Caribbean diving with two genuinely wild seasons stitched in. Winter brings pregnant bull sharks to a 24-metre sand flat off Playa and sailfish to the blue; summer stacks hundreds of whale sharks offshore. Between them you drift warm, easy reefs on the Yucatán Current, land on hurricane-split wrecks, and tour the sculpture galleries of MUSA. Short boat rides and year-round warm water suit everyone from new divers to shark-focused veterans.

Cancún & Playa del Carmen is at its best Nov-Mar & Jul-Aug, with Jan, Feb, Jul, Aug and Dec rating as peak season. Expect tropical water of 26–29°C (suit: 3mm full year-round), visibility up to ~25m, and moderate currents — Drift-style boat diving on the Playa reefs; strong current possible on the deep wrecks. Divers here work 7 recognised sites — 3 reefs, 2 wrecks, 1 artificial reef and 1 drift dive — ranging from MUSA — Manchones (8-10m) for beginner divers up to Los Arcos (25-30m), advanced territory.

Currents detailThe north-flowing Yucatán Current sweeps the coast, so most Playa del Carmen reef diving is done as relaxed one-way drifts with the boat following. Inshore Cancún sites like Manchones and MUSA sit in protected shallows with little water movement, while the deeper wrecks can carry genuinely strong flow.
What to pack here

Water sits around 28°C here, so a 3 mm wetsuit is the right call. A 5 mm is worth packing for winter or deeper, repeat dives.
