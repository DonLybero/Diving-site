Gili Islands & Lombok

Indonesia · Southeast Asia — Reliable year-round turtles over easy reefs, ideal for beginners

Photo: Tilonaut (CC BY 3.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Three small coral-fringed islets off Lombok's northwest coast, the Gilis deliver Indonesia at its most relaxed: warm, easy reefs where green and hawksbill turtles are all but guaranteed - Turtle Heaven stacks them on a single pinnacle. Short day-boat rides reach sloping reefs, the coral-crusted Bounty Wreck and gentle walls like Meno's, with drift and channel sites adding pace when the current runs. It is an ideal place to learn or ease back into diving.

Gili Islands & Lombok is at its best May-Sep, with May, Jun, Jul, Aug and Sep rating as peak season. Expect tropical water of 27–29°C (suit: 3mm), visibility up to ~32m, and medium currents — Lombok Strait drift common; training sites gentle. Divers here work 8 recognised sites — 2 reefs, 2 pinnacles, 1 wall, 1 wreck, 1 drift dive and 1 muck dive — ranging from Meno Wall (5-20m) for beginner divers up to Deep Turbo (18-32m), advanced territory.

What to expect down there

Expect warm, unhurried diving: 27-29°C year-round in a 3mm suit, over sloping reefs and coral heads that rarely demand more than easy finning. When the Lombok Strait current runs, Halik's ridges and canyons turn into a proper drift, and Deep Turbo drops you onto sea-fan-covered seamounts at 24-32 m with garden eels rippling over the sand. Han's Reef swaps scenery for critter hunting on the rubble. The signature moment is a green turtle lifting off a coral head close enough to fill your mask.

What you’ll encounter

Green and hawksbill turtles are the constant, grazing, sleeping and queuing at cleaning stations at nearly every site. Whitetip reef sharks rest under ledges and patrol the deeper slopes, cuttlefish hover and flare over the rubble, and Han's Reef delivers seahorses, frogfish, ribbon eels and octopus. The dry months from May to September bring the clearest water and the busiest reefs.

The honest picture

What can change on the day?

  • Seasons drift. Marine-life timing shifts year to year with plankton blooms and lunar cycles — a peak landing a couple of weeks early or late is normal, not bad luck.
  • Temperatures are ranges. Water figures here are typical monthly ranges (±1°C); an upwelling or a heatwave can step outside them.
  • Visibility is seasonal, not daily. Wind, swell, rain and plankton move it day to day — read our metres as the month's typical form, not a promise for your dive.
  • Currents can run above the rating. Conditions on the day decide — brief with your operator before the first dive, and sit one out if it's beyond your training.
  • A score compares, it doesn't guarantee. We rank months from dive-operator and liveaboard calendars so you can weigh destinations like for like — always confirm current conditions with a local dive centre.
