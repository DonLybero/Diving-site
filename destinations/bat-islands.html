Bat Islands

Costa Rica · Eastern Pacific / Costa Rica — Costa Rica's premier bull shark dive: at the Big Scare (El Gran Susto) a dozen or more 2.5-3.5m bull sharks cruise volcanic pinnacles at 20-30m, joined by giant Pacific mantas, squadrons of eagle and cow-nose rays and dense schools of jacks and snapper. A committing offshore day-boat trip from the Guanacaste coast, best in the warm green season.

Photo: Holobionics (CC BY-SA 4.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Bat Islands is at its best Jun-Oct, with Jun, Jul, Aug and Sep rating as peak season. Expect tropical Pacific (upwelling-influenced) water of 24–29°C (suit: 5mm full (thermoclines can plunge below 21°C even when the surface is warm)), visibility up to ~22m, and strong currents — Exposed offshore pinnacles with strong, variable currents, surge and downcurrents around the rock; the ~45km open-water crossing from Playas del Coco can be rough. Advanced certification and solid buoyancy are expected. Divers here work 6 recognised sites — 1 rocky pinnacle, 1 volcanic pinnacle, 1 point / rocky reef, 1 rocky reef, 1 boulder reef and 1 wall / slope — ranging from Coral Gardens (8-18m) for intermediate divers up to Bat Island Wall (18-30m), advanced territory.

Recognised dive sites (6)

Depths are typical published ranges — always confirm with your operator.

SiteTypeDepthLevelWhy it’s known
Big Scare (El Gran Susto)rocky pinnacle18-27madvancedThe signature site: exposed rocks breaking the surface where six to a dozen large bull sharks patrol the volcanic pinnacles, joined by giant mantas, jacks, snapper and the occasional sailfish or marlin.
Black Rock (Bajo Negro)volcanic pinnacle20-30madvancedA steep pinnacle rising from around 30m toward the surface off Santa Rosa, drawing squadrons of eagle rays, dense schooling fish, occasional bull sharks and cruising manta rays.
Punta Gordapoint / rocky reef12-25madvancedA rocky point known for gliding groups of common and spotted eagle rays over the boulders, with turtles and reef fish in shallower water.
The Pointrocky reef12-24madvancedCurrent-swept ridges and boulder gardens holding schooling grunts, snapper and pompano, with rays and turtles working the edges.
Coral Gardensboulder reef8-18mintermediateA slightly more sheltered rocky-reef area used when conditions are rougher, with morays, invertebrates and reef fish and lighter current than the sharky pinnacles.
Bat Island Wallwall / slope18-30madvancedA sloping wall along the islands where big pelagic schools, rays and passing bull sharks show up in the current, best dived on slack.
