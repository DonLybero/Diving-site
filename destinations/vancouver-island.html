Vancouver Island

Canada · Pacific Northwest — Cold-water Pacific - wolf eels, giant octopus, sea lions

Photo: Christoph Strässler from Oberdorf BL, Schweiz (CC BY-SA 4.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Cold, current-fed Pacific water turns this British Columbia coastline into an invertebrate riot: Browning Wall is carpeted stem to stern in soft corals and anemones, wolf eels and giant Pacific octopus den in the rocks, and Steller sea lions buzz divers at Race Rocks. Big artificial reefs like HMCS Saskatchewan add wreck steel. Slack-window boat diving in a drysuit, at its clearest October through March, with Ogden Point offering an easy shore option.

Vancouver Island is at its best Oct-Mar (best viz), with Jan, Feb, Oct, Nov and Dec rating as peak season. Expect saltwater of 7–16°C (suit: Drysuit only (cold year-round)), visibility up to ~25m, and strong currents — Tidal rapids; current-timed sites. Divers here work 9 recognised sites — 3 wrecks, 2 walls, 2 drift dives, 1 reef and 1 shore dive — ranging from Ogden Point Breakwater (5-18m) for beginner divers up to Nakwakto Rapids (Turret Rock) (10-22m), advanced territory.
