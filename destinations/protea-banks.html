Protea Banks

South Africa · Indian Ocean / South Africa — South Africa's shark-diving capital: a deep 27-40 m reef of caves, pinnacles and gullies where Zambezi (bull) sharks patrol year-round, wintering ragged-tooth sharks pack the caves, and summer brings tiger sharks and schools of scalloped hammerheads. The sardine run passes offshore in June-July. Strictly for experienced, current-comfortable divers.

Photo: Willemferguson2 (CC BY-SA 4.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Protea Banks starts where most reefs end: a deep offshore reef of caves, pinnacles and gullies lying 27-40 m down off South Africa's KwaZulu-Natal coast, dived as fast blue-water drifts with negative entries and open-water ascents. Zambezi (bull) sharks patrol year-round, wintering ragged-tooth sharks pack the caves, and summer adds tiger sharks and schooling hammerheads, with the sardine run streaming past in June and July. This is uncompromising, experienced-divers-only territory.

Protea Banks is at its best Jun-Sep & Nov-Dec, with Jun, Jul, Aug, Sep, Nov and Dec rating as peak season. Expect warm-temperate to subtropical Indian Ocean water of 20–26°C (suit: 5mm full year-round), visibility up to ~25m, and strong currents — Offshore fossil-dune reef 7-8 km out swept by the Agulhas Current; drift dives are the norm and current can run hard north or south, sometimes shifting mid-dive. Divers here work 6 recognised sites — 2 reef / drifts, 1 cave / gully, 1 cave / reef, 1 gully / reef and 1 sand / gully — ranging from Northern Pinnacles (28-34m) for advanced divers up to Sand Shark Gully (The Arena) (40m), advanced territory.
