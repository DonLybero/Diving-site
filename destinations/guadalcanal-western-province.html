Guadalcanal & Western Province

Solomon Islands · South Pacific — WWII wrecks and pristine reefs with rich macro life

Photo: Xplore Dive from Mooloolaba, Australia (CC BY 2.0) via Wikimedia Commons

The Solomon Islands wear their WWII history underwater: beached Japanese transports at Bonegi drop from shore-entry shallows to 55 meters, an intact B-17 bomber rests off Honiara, and Gizo's Toa Maru ranks among the Pacific's finest recreational wrecks. Out in the Western Province, reefs like Njari's Grand Central Station have logged 270-plus fish species on a single dive. Mild currents, mandarinfish macro, and empty sites make this a rewarding resort or liveaboard trip, April through November.

Guadalcanal & Western Province is at its best Apr-Nov, with Jun, Jul, Aug, Sep and Oct rating as peak season. Expect tropical water of 28–29°C (suit: 3mm), visibility up to ~32m, and low currents — Fairly easy diving, not much current. Divers here work 9 recognised sites — 7 wrecks, 1 reef and 1 shark dive — ranging from Hirokawa Maru (Bonegi 1) (5-55m) for beginner divers up to USS John Penn (35-58m), technical-diving territory.
