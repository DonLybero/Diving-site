Bonaire

Caribbean (Netherlands) · Caribbean — World's best shore-diving with healthy fringing reefs, dive-when-you-want freedom

Photo: SNORKELINGDIVES.COM (CC BY 2.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Bonaire perfected drive-and-dive freedom: rent a truck, load tanks, and roll into any of the marked sites along the leeward west coast whenever you like. The fringing reef starts meters from shore and stays remarkably healthy, from the fish-thick house reefs off Kralendijk to the sponge-wrapped pilings of Salt Pier and the shore-accessible Hilma Hooker wreck at 30 m. Calm, easy conditions make it ideal for new divers and macro hunters chasing frogfish and seahorses.

Bonaire is at its best Apr-Jun & Oct-Dec, with Mar, Apr, May and Jun rating as peak season. Expect tropical water of 26–29°C (suit: 3mm), visibility up to ~32m, and low currents — Protected leeward shore diving, minimal current. Divers here work 8 recognised sites — 5 shore dives, 1 wreck, 1 reef and 1 wall — ranging from 1000 Steps (6-30m) for beginner divers up to Forest (Klein Bonaire) (12-30m), intermediate territory.

What to expect down there

Entries are as simple as diving gets: stride off a dock or wade over rubble, cross a sandy shelf, and the terraced coral begins around 6 m before rolling down past 25 m. Current on the leeward coast is near nil, the water holds 26-29°C, and visibility runs 25 m and better through the calm months. The set-piece dives are structural, the Hilma Hooker's 72 m hull lying on her side at 30 m and Salt Pier's pilings furred in sponge and shafted with light.

What you’ll encounter

Turtles are the constant, grazing the shore reefs and cruising the black-coral wall at Forest off Klein Bonaire, while tarpon hold in the shadows under piers. Slow searching on the sandy slopes near the marina turns up seahorses, frogfish and swaying garden eels. Bari Reef carries the Caribbean's highest recorded fish counts, eagle rays pass the drop in spring, and late-summer nights bring coral spawning.

The honest picture

What can change on the day?

  • Seasons drift. Marine-life timing shifts year to year with plankton blooms and lunar cycles — a peak landing a couple of weeks early or late is normal, not bad luck.
  • Temperatures are ranges. Water figures here are typical monthly ranges (±1°C); an upwelling or a heatwave can step outside them.
  • Visibility is seasonal, not daily. Wind, swell, rain and plankton move it day to day — read our metres as the month's typical form, not a promise for your dive.
  • Currents can run above the rating. Conditions on the day decide — brief with your operator before the first dive, and sit one out if it's beyond your training.
  • A score compares, it doesn't guarantee. We rank months from dive-operator and liveaboard calendars so you can weigh destinations like for like — always confirm current conditions with a local dive centre.
