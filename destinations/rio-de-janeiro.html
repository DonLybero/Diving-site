Rio de Janeiro

Find stays near Rio de Janeiro →

Brazil's most visited city keeps a genuine dive scene on its doorstep: the Cagarras Islands, a federally protected natural monument where fishing is banned, rise just 5 km off Ipanema beach. Boats run from Marina da Gloria downtown and from Barra da Tijuca — mostly at weekends — to sheltered channels like Ressurreta, the easy turtle reefs of the Ilhas Tijucas, and, for experienced divers, Ilha Rasa's walls to 42 m and the scattered wreck of the steamer Buenos Aires, lost there in the early 1890s.

Rio de Janeiro is at its best Oct-Mar, with Jan, Feb, Mar and Dec rating as peak season. Expect subtropical Atlantic water of 22–26°C (suit: 5mm most of the year), visibility up to ~15m, and mild to moderate currents — Sheltered channels like Ressurreta are easy; the outer sites — Ilha Rasa and the Buenos Aires wreck — commonly carry surface current, so descents go down the line. Divers here work 5 recognised sites — 1 island reef / drop-off, 1 sheltered channel reef, 1 offshore island / wall, 1 wreck and 1 island reef — ranging from Ressurreta (8-12m) for beginner divers up to Naufrágio Buenos Aires (12-42m), advanced territory.

What to expect down there

The contrast is the appeal: drift a sheltered channel at Ressurreta over easy rock at 8-12 m, or drop the line at Ilha Rasa where walls fall from 8 m past 40 m and conditions swing hard — visibility from 2 m to 20 m, water from 15°C to 23°C. The Buenos Aires lies broken from 12 m down to its propeller near 42 m, usually under surface current. Summer brings 25-26°C water and the clearest outer-island days; winter fronts cool the sea to around 22°C and push swell into the exposed sites.

What you’ll encounter

Green turtles graze the Tijucas reefs and southern stingrays settle on the sand between the boulders, with dusky grouper, morays and octopus resident on the rocky structure year-round. Dolphins visit the protected Cagarras archipelago through the year, and passing pelagics sweep the drop-offs in season. Summer concentrates the schools around the islands; winter leaves the residents to carry quieter, cooler dives.
