Galapagos Islands

Ecuador · Eastern Pacific — Big-animal mecca; whale sharks & hammerhead schools at Darwin/Wolf.

Photo: Paul and Jill (CC BY 2.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Converging ocean currents collide at this volcanic archipelago 1,000 kilometers off Ecuador, stacking cold, nutrient-loaded water against the equator, which is why nothing else dives like the Galapagos. Liveaboards push north to Darwin and Wolf, where whale sharks cruise past walls of scalloped hammerheads from June to November, while central sites add playful sea lions, mola mola, and feeding marine iguanas. Expect negative entries, down-currents, and surge: this is advanced diving, and worth every degree of chill.

Galapagos Islands is at its best Jun-Nov (big animals) / Dec-May (calm), with Jun, Jul, Aug, Sep and Oct rating as peak season. Expect temperate-tropical water of 21–26°C (suit: 5-7mm + hood/gloves), visibility up to ~24m, and strong currents — Humboldt/Cromwell currents; Darwin/Wolf advanced only. Divers here work 8 recognised sites — 2 shark dives, 2 walls, 2 reefs, 1 pinnacle and 1 drift dive — ranging from Kicker Rock (León Dormido) (10-30m) for intermediate divers up to Cabo Marshall (10-30m), advanced territory.

What to pack here

Water sits around 23°C here, so a 5 mm wetsuit is the right call. A 7 mm is worth packing for winter or deeper, repeat dives.
