Dubai & Fujairah

United Arab Emirates · Gulf of Oman / United Arab Emirates — Easy year-round diving on the Gulf of Oman east coast — turtle-packed reefs at Dibba Rock and Snoopy Island, soft-coral-crusted Martini Rock, the purpose-sunk Inchcape wrecks, plus Dubai's artificial-reef wreck sites, with summer whale shark visits.

Photo: Wongleejean (CC BY-SA 4.0) via Wikimedia Commons

The UAE's real diving lives on the Fujairah side, where the Gulf of Oman's plankton-rich water feeds rocky reefs at Dibba Rock, Snoopy Island and Martini Rock, plus the purpose-sunk Inchcape wrecks; Dubai adds sandy-bottom wreck sites and artificial reefs in the Gulf. Expect warm, easy conditions with modest visibility that peaks in winter and can green out during summer blooms — when whale sharks arrive. Ideal for new divers, wreck fans and layover diving.

Dubai & Fujairah is at its best Oct-May, with Jan, Feb, Mar, Nov and Dec rating as peak season. Expect tropical (very warm in summer) water of 23–32°C (suit: 3mm full Oct-May (5mm midwinter for the cold-prone)), visibility up to ~16m, and low currents — mostly slack; light current or surge on exposed sites. Divers here work 7 recognised sites — 3 reefs, 3 wrecks and 1 artificial reef — ranging from Dibba Rock (5-14m) for beginner divers up to Zainab Wreck (22-30m), advanced territory.

What to expect down there

Expect warm, easy diving with honest visibility - 6 to 16 m by season. On the Fujairah side, Martini Rock rises as a pinnacle crusted in orange soft coral, while the Inchcape wrecks sit upright at 23 to 32 m, busy with morays and batfish. Over in Dubai, the Zainab tanker lies on her port side in a swarm of barracuda and snapper, and the Car Cemetery is sunken vehicles turned reef. Water swings from 23°C to a bathwater 32°C in summer.

What you’ll encounter

Green turtles are the everyday sight at Dibba Rock, joined by patrolling blacktip reef sharks and honeycomb morays gaping from the wrecks. The headline visitor is the whale shark, cruising the reefs through the hot summer months when the water greens out with plankton. Come winter, pharaoh cuttlefish move in to court in the shallows and macro life crowds Martini Rock at its densest.

The honest picture

What can change on the day?

  • Seasons drift. Marine-life timing shifts year to year with plankton blooms and lunar cycles — a peak landing a couple of weeks early or late is normal, not bad luck.
  • Temperatures are ranges. Water figures here are typical monthly ranges (±1°C); an upwelling or a heatwave can step outside them.
  • Visibility is seasonal, not daily. Wind, swell, rain and plankton move it day to day — read our metres as the month's typical form, not a promise for your dive.
  • Currents can run above the rating. Conditions on the day decide — brief with your operator before the first dive, and sit one out if it's beyond your training.
  • A score compares, it doesn't guarantee. We rank months from dive-operator and liveaboard calendars so you can weigh destinations like for like — always confirm current conditions with a local dive centre.
