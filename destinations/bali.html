Bali

Indonesia · Coral Triangle / Indonesia — A coral-encrusted WWII wreck you can walk into off a pebble beach, world-class black-sand muck at Seraya and Padang Bai, and Menjangan's sheer gorgonian walls — all shore- or short-boat accessible year-round.

Photo: Lakshmi Sawitri (CC BY 2.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Bali's mainland coast packs remarkable variety into short taxi rides: the coral-carpeted USAT Liberty wreck at Tulamben, black-sand muck slopes at Seraya and Amed, critter-rich bays at Padang Bai, and Menjangan's crystalline walls in the northwest. Warm, mostly gentle water and shore entries make it a superb place to build skills, while the macro life keeps seasoned photographers busy for weeks. Dry-season months bring the flattest seas and clearest water.

Bali is at its best Apr-Jul & Oct-Nov, with Apr, May, Jun, Jul, Oct and Nov rating as peak season. Expect tropical water of 26–29°C (suit: 3mm full year-round), visibility up to ~30m, and low currents — calm shore sites; channel sites near Padang Bai can run. Divers here work 6 recognised sites — 2 walls, 1 wreck, 1 muck dive, 1 reef and 1 wall / artificial reef — ranging from USAT Liberty, Tulamben (5-30m) for beginner divers up to Tulamben Drop-Off (5-40m), intermediate territory.

Currents detailThe northeast coast at Tulamben and Amed is sheltered, with gentle currents that rarely trouble shore entries. Sites in the Padang Bai channel feel the Lombok Strait's pull and can switch on quickly, so guides time slack windows there.
What to pack here

Water sits around 29°C here, so a 3 mm wetsuit is the right call. A 5 mm is worth packing for winter or deeper, repeat dives.
