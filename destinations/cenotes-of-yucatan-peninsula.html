Cenotes of Yucatán Peninsula

Mexico · North America — Crystal-clear freshwater caverns with stalactites, haloclines and dramatic light beams

Photo: Offthebeatonpath (CC BY-SA 4.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Beneath the Yucatan's jungle, rain has dissolved the limestone into vast flooded cave systems, and cenotes are the sinkhole windows that let divers in. Expect gin-clear fresh water with virtually no current, cathedral light beams, stalactite-hung passages and haloclines that blur vision like oil in water. Guided cavern lines at Dos Ojos and Gran Cenote welcome any certified diver; El Pit and Angelita, with their eerie sulfide clouds at 30 m, reward deeper experience.

Cenotes of Yucatán Peninsula is at its best Nov-Mar, with Jan, Feb, Mar, Nov and Dec rating as peak season. Expect freshwater of 25°C (suit: 5mm), visibility up to ~60m, and low currents — Calm still water, no current or waves. Divers here work 10 recognised sites — 6 cenotes and 4 caverns — ranging from Cenote Dos Ojos (6-10m) for beginner divers up to Cenote Zapote (Hells Bells) (max 30m), advanced territory.
