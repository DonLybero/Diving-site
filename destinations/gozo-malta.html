Gozo & Malta

Malta · Mediterranean — Clear-water wrecks, caverns and famous Gozo shore dives

Photo: Diego Delso (CC BY-SA 4.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Few places make shore diving this easy. The Maltese islands drop straight into clear blue Mediterranean water, and most of the classics, from the Blue Hole at Dwejra to the Cirkewwa arch to the scuttled tanker Um El Faroud, are reached by walking in with your kit. Limestone shapes the character here: caverns, chimneys and light-shafted grottoes at every level, plus deliberately sunk wrecks deep enough to reward advanced divers. May through October brings warm, settled conditions.

Gozo & Malta is at its best May-Oct, with Jun, Jul, Aug and Sep rating as peak season. Expect saltwater of 15–26°C (suit: 5mm summer, 7mm shoulder/winter), visibility up to ~30m, and low currents — Mild currents, beginner-friendly shore diving. Divers here work 10 recognised sites — 5 wrecks, 2 caverns, 2 archs and 1 cave — ranging from Blue Hole (Dwejra) (5-25m) for beginner divers up to Double Arch Reef (18-36m), advanced territory.

What to expect down there

Shore entries lead straight into sculpted rock: an 80 m tunnel runs from the Inland Sea out to a sheer drop-off, and two natural arches stack on top of each other off Xwejni Bay. Scuttled steel adds structure — the P29 patrol boat sits upright at 35 m and the Karwela's famous staircase waits near 40 m. Visibility rarely drops below 20 m in any month; the sea runs 26°C in August but only 15°C by late winter.

What you’ll encounter

Groupers hold territory on the wrecks and ledges, barracuda school thickest over the reefs in high summer when tuna flash past the drop-offs, and octopus work the rubble year-round. This is subtle Mediterranean life rather than big-animal diving: nudibranchs carpet the walls through winter, and seahorses turn up in the seagrass in the quiet December weeks.

The honest picture

What can change on the day?

  • Seasons drift. Marine-life timing shifts year to year with plankton blooms and lunar cycles — a peak landing a couple of weeks early or late is normal, not bad luck.
  • Temperatures are ranges. Water figures here are typical monthly ranges (±1°C); an upwelling or a heatwave can step outside them.
  • Visibility is seasonal, not daily. Wind, swell, rain and plankton move it day to day — read our metres as the month's typical form, not a promise for your dive.
  • Currents can run above the rating. Conditions on the day decide — brief with your operator before the first dive, and sit one out if it's beyond your training.
  • A score compares, it doesn't guarantee. We rank months from dive-operator and liveaboard calendars so you can weigh destinations like for like — always confirm current conditions with a local dive centre.
