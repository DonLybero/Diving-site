Chania (Crete)

Greece · Mediterranean / Greece — Western Crete's main dive base, best known for the submerged Elephant Cave (Elefas) at Cape Drepano with its fossil stalactite-and-stalagmite wall and Ice Age elephant-bone finds. Clear blue Aegean/Mediterranean water, dramatic caverns, walls and reefs, plus a few wreck dives, wrapped around a long April-to-November summer season and quiet, cool winters.

Photo: dronepicr (CC BY 2.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Western Crete anchors its diving in Chania, where day boats and easy shore entries work a coastline of clear Aegean water, caverns and drop-offs. The headline act is the Elephant Cave at Cape Drepano, a chamber walled with fused stalactites and stalagmites where Ice Age dwarf-elephant bones were found. Around it sit sheltered training reefs, the Lighthouse Wall and grouper-thick boulder slopes off Rodopou, a mix that suits new and experienced divers alike across a long June-to-October prime season.

Chania (Crete) is at its best Jun-Oct, with Jul, Aug and Sep rating as peak season. Expect temperate saltwater (Mediterranean/Aegean) water of 16–26°C (suit: 5mm), visibility up to ~32m, and mild currents — Generally sheltered water on the north coast; mild currents possible around exposed capes, islets and the cave entrances, stronger with meltemi wind. Divers here work 7 recognised sites — 4 reefs, 2 caverns and 1 wall — ranging from Islet of Marathi (6-20 m) for beginner divers up to Dia Island (8-30 m), intermediate territory.

Recognised dive sites (7)

Depths are typical published ranges — always confirm with your operator.

SiteTypeDepthLevelWhy it’s known
Elephant Cave (Elefas)cavern10-30 mintermediateChania's signature dive at Cape Drepano off Souda Bay. The entrance sits around 10 m and opens into a large chamber with a spectacular fused stalactite-and-stalagmite wall; fossilised bones of an extinct dwarf elephant were found here. Broad and shallow with air pockets, but still an overhead environment for trained divers.
Menies (Diktynna)reef8-25 mintermediateReef and boulder dive off the remote Menies coast on the Rodopou peninsula, near the ancient Diktynna sanctuary. Clear water, sloping rock terraces and good numbers of grouper, moray and octopus.
Islet of Marathireef6-20 mbeginnerSheltered, scenic reef dive off the Akrotiri peninsula with easy access and crystal-clear water, ideal for training and relaxed exploration among rocky reef and seagrass.
Ammoudireef5-18 mbeginnerEasy shore and shallow reef dive in some of the clearest turquoise water in the area; gentle rocky reef with damselfish, octopus and nudibranchs, popular for check-out and refresher dives.
Lighthouse Wallwall12-35 mintermediateA dramatic drop-off wall dive, one of the finer walls in the Chania area, with cracks and overhangs sheltering grouper and moray and passing barracuda in the blue.
Seal Cavecavern8-25 mintermediateA rocky cavern dive named for the monk seals occasionally seen along this coast; swim-throughs and shafts of light with cavern-dwelling life on the walls.
Dia Islandreef8-30 mintermediateBoat dive around the uninhabited, Natura 2000-protected islet off Crete's north coast, with exceptionally clear water, healthy reef and wall terrain and depths to suit both newer and experienced divers.
