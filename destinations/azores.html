Azores

Portugal · North Atlantic / Europe — Atlantic pelagic diving - blue/mako sharks and mantas at seamounts

Photo: Eduardo Manchon (CC BY-SA 3.0) via Wikimedia Commons

Nine volcanic Portuguese islands anchor the mid-Atlantic, and their diving happens in serious blue water. Offshore seamounts like Princess Alice Bank draw squadrons of mobula rays, Condor Bank stages blue and mako shark encounters, and visibility routinely tops 30 meters. Currents run strong out there, so the banks are boat trips for confident divers, though coastal wrecks and Terceira's anchor graveyard offer easier days. July through October is prime season.

Azores is at its best Jul-Oct, with Jul, Aug and Sep rating as peak season. Expect saltwater of 17–24°C (suit: 5-7mm wetsuit), visibility up to ~30m, and strong currents — Princess Alice strong currents, experienced only. Divers here work 8 recognised sites — 2 seamounts, 2 reefs, 2 wrecks, 1 pinnacle and 1 cave — ranging from Dori Wreck (9-20m) for beginner divers up to Gruta dos Camarões (35-45m), technical-diving territory.

What to expect down there

This is blue-water diving: a long run offshore, a descent down the line with nothing but open Atlantic beneath you, and a seamount top materialising at 35-40 m in visibility that regularly hits 30 m. Currents push hard across Princess Alice and Condor Banks, so you hold the line and let the pelagics come to you. Water climbs from 17°C in winter to about 24°C in late summer; closer in, the Dori wreck at 9-20 m and Terceira's anchor-strewn seabed give calmer, shallower days.

What you’ll encounter

Summer is the show: blue sharks work the banks from July into September, makos join them at Condor, and squadrons of mobula rays school over Ambrósio and Princess Alice from late spring. Mola mola drift past the seamounts and dolphins often escort the boats out. Through winter the pelagics thin, leaving groupers and morays holding the volcanic ledges.
