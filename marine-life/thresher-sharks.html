Thresher Sharks

Dawn on the seamount, where the whip-tails come to be cleaned.

The pelagic thresher is all elegance — a slender shark with an upper tail lobe as long as its body, which it cracks like a whip to stun fish. It spends its days in cool deep water and rises at dawn to seamount cleaning stations, where divers wait in the half-light for it to glide in. It is one of very few reliably-scheduled encounters with a truly open-ocean shark.

Good to know
  • It is a dawn dive — be on the seamount before sunrise when the threshers rise to be cleaned.
  • Stay behind the agreed line and keep still; threshers are shy and veer off at the first chase.
  • Cooler, deeper cleaning stations mean solid buoyancy and often a 5 mm suit.
