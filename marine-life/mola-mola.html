Mola Mola

The strangest giant in the sea, up from the cold.

The mola mola — ocean sunfish — is the heaviest bony fish alive, a two-tonne silver disc that looks like a swimming head. It spends most of its life deep and cold, rising to reef cleaning stations where bannerfish pick off its parasites. Sightings ride cold upwellings, so mola season is a cool-water affair: thermoclines, a thicker suit, and a slow, patient hover while the giant is groomed.

Good to know
  • Mola ride cold upwellings — expect sharp thermoclines and pack extra exposure protection.
  • Approach slow and low; a spooked mola drops straight back into the deep.
  • They surface at cleaning stations, so find the cleaner fish and you often find the mola.
