# World Diving Calendar — 24 Periods (Bi-Monthly)

A year-round planning guide for 11 world-class diving destinations, built from a wide-net web search of water temperature, currents, and seasonal marine life. The year is divided into **24 periods** — each month split into an *early* half (≈1st–15th) and a *late* half (≈16th–end).

**Destinations covered:** Red Sea (Egypt) · Seychelles · Raja Ampat · Galápagos · Sipadan · Maldives · Palau · Great Barrier Reef · Truk Lagoon · Cocos Island · Komodo National Park.

> Temperatures are sea-surface in °C unless flagged as at-depth/thermocline. Marine-life peak weeks shift year-to-year with plankton blooms and lunar cycles — local operators give the definitive near-term word.

**Anchor signals to remember**
- **Sipadan is CLOSED the entire month of November** (Sabah Parks conservation).
- **Raja Ampat low season is roughly July–August** (SE monsoon; many liveaboards pause/reposition).
- **Galápagos / Cocos big-animal peak = Aug–Oct** (cold, rough, low viz — but whale sharks + hammerhead walls).
- **GBR dwarf minke whales = June–July only** (Ribbon Reefs); **coral spawning = November** after the full moon.
- **Maldives flips by monsoon**: Dec–Apr = clear-water + hammerheads/tigers; May–Nov = manta/whale-shark "plankton soup" (Hanifaru peak Jul–Oct).
- **Komodo mantas are inverted by zone**: North best in dry season (Apr–Oct), South best in wet season (Nov–Mar, peak Jan–Feb).

## PART 2 — Destination Detail Sheets

### 1. Red Sea (Egypt)
**Water temp:** Feb low ~22 °C → Aug high ~30 °C (shallows). Thermocline a few °C cooler in summer.
**Wetsuit:** 3 mm summer · 5–7 mm winter.
**Currents:** Sheltered bays mild; offshore marine-park reefs (**Brothers, Daedalus, Elphinstone**) have strong, unpredictable drift-dive currents. Winter northerly winds can cancel exposed day-trips (e.g. Thistlegorm).
**Marine life by period:** Big-fish season opens Mar–Apr (mantas, early whale sharks) · **whale shark peak Jun–Jul** · **hammerhead peak Jul–Aug** (Daedalus/Brothers) · coral spawning Aug–Sep · **oceanic whitetip + thresher peak Oct–Nov** · winter = reef/wreck focus, clearest viz, threshers. Spinner dolphins year-round (Sha'ab El Erg).
**Best windows:** **Mar–May & Sep–Nov** (warm, calm, big marine activity). Summer = warmest/calmest crossings for deep-south liveaboards. Winter = best viz for wrecks.

### 2. Seychelles
**Water temp:** Jul–Aug low ~26 °C → Apr high ~30 °C. Thermocline ~24 °C at depth Jul–Aug.
**Wetsuit:** 3 mm year-round (add vest Jul–Aug).
**Currents:** Inner islands (Mahé/Praslin) mild & beginner-friendly. SE-trade season (May–Oct) brings 20–30 kt winds, surge on exposed coasts, and upwelling that feeds pelagics. Outer islands/Aldabra = liveaboard/permit only.
**Marine life:** **Whale sharks Aug–Nov (peak Oct–Nov)** around Mahé + **Apr–May Amirantes** · **manta peak Oct–Nov** · hawksbill/green turtle nesting Oct–Mar · reef sharks, eagle rays, nurse sharks year-round.
**Best windows:** **April & October–November** (transition months: flat seas, 25–35 m viz, pelagics present).

### 3. Raja Ampat (Indonesia)
**Water temp:** Feb low ~28 °C → Oct–Dec high ~30 °C. Occasional thermocline ~22 °C (deep Misool).
**Wetsuit:** 3 mm.
**Currents:** Drift diving central; strongest at Dampier Strait sites (Cape Kri, Sardine Reef, Blue Magic) where tidal flow drives nutrient upwellings. Plankton/current build from May (SE monsoon).
**Marine life:** **Manta peak Dec–Mar** (Jan–Feb strongest) at Manta Sandy/Ridge · year-round wobbegongs, epaulette "walking" sharks, pygmy seahorses, record reef biodiversity. Macro & night diving shine May–Sep.
**Best windows:** **Oct–Apr** (calm, viz >30 m; Dec–Feb peak). **Jul–Aug = low season** (rough, many liveaboards pause/reposition to Komodo or Cenderawasih).

### 4. Galápagos Islands (Ecuador)
**Water temp:** Central islands Aug–Sep low ~20 °C → Feb–Apr high ~25 °C. **Western islands (Isabela/Fernandina) down to ~16–18 °C**; Darwin/Wolf cool-season ~22–26 °C with cold thermoclines.
**Wetsuit:** **5–7 mm + hood/gloves** (esp. Jun–Nov & west).
**Currents:** Notoriously strong — four converging currents (Humboldt, Cromwell, Panama, S. Equatorial). Negative entries, down-currents, surge. Darwin & Wolf advanced-only, liveaboard-only; Gordon's Rocks = "the washing machine."
**Marine life:** **Whale sharks Jun–Nov (peak Aug–Sep)** at Darwin · **biggest hammerhead schools Jun–Nov** · mantas Dec–May · mola mola Aug–Dec · sea lions, marine iguanas, penguins, red-lipped batfish year-round.
**Best windows:** **Jun–Nov** big animals (cold/rough/low viz) · **Dec–May** warm, calm, mantas, better viz. Liveaboards run year-round; Jul–Oct books ~12 mo ahead.

### 5. Sipadan Island (Malaysia)
**Water temp:** 27–30 °C year-round (coolest Dec–Feb, warmest Apr–Jul). Thermocline a couple °C below ~20–25 m.
**Wetsuit:** 3 mm (5 mm for deep repeats in cooler months).
**Currents:** The key variable — stronger current = more big-fish action. Barracuda Point (chevron barracuda tornado, dawn hammerheads), South Point (strongest, pelagics), Drop Off (gentle house reef).
**Marine life:** Mostly resident — barracuda tornado, jackfish, reef sharks, bumphead parrotfish, **green/hawksbill turtles (20+/dive)**; turtle nesting Apr–Sep. Occasional hammerheads (dawn), whale sharks, thresher.
**Best windows:** **Mar–Jun** (calmest, viz 20–50 m) & **Sep–Oct** (2nd window). **⛔ Island CLOSED all November.** Dec–Feb softer viz with north winds; Aug–early Sep south winds dip viz.

### 6. Maldives
**Water temp:** 27–30 °C (coolest Dec–Jan, warmest Apr; far south to ~24 °C in NE monsoon). Thermoclines below ~20 m.
**Wetsuit:** 3 mm (5 mm far south / deep repeats).
**Currents:** Monsoon-driven channel ("kandu") drift diving. **NE monsoon (Dec–Apr):** dive the eastern (incoming/clear) side for viz & pelagics, mantas on the west. **SW monsoon (May–Nov):** dive the western (incoming) side, mantas/whale sharks gather east. Strongest currents at full/new moon.
**Marine life:** **Hammerheads Rasdhoo Dec–May** (dawn) · **Fuvahmulah tiger sharks Dec–Mar** · **Hanifaru Bay manta + whale-shark aggregation peak Jul–Oct** · whale sharks resident S. Ari MPA (best Jul–Nov) · grey reef sharks, eagle rays, nurse sharks year-round.
**Best windows:** **Dec–Apr** clear water + hammerheads/tigers (high season) · **May–Nov** mantas/whale sharks (Hanifaru), lower viz, low season prices.

### 7. Palau (Micronesia)
**Water temp:** 28–30 °C (Feb low ~28 °C, May–Jun high ~30 °C). Mixed layer to ~40 m; cooler thermoclines on deep walls.
**Wetsuit:** 3 mm.
**Currents:** Tidal, strongest at full/new moon. **Blue Corner** (reef hooks essential, viz 28 m+ on incoming) · **Ulong Channel** (high-speed drift) · **German Channel** (gentle, manta cleaning) · **Peleliu** (dangerous down-currents, dry-season only).
**Marine life:** Lunar spawning aggregations — **red snapper (full moon), bumphead parrotfish (new moon), moorish idol (Jan–Feb full moon)**, mostly Feb–Apr · **grey-reef shark peak Mar–May** · mantas Dec–Apr (German Channel) · whale sharks Nov–Apr · Jellyfish Lake (Nov–Apr, *population currently diminished*).
**Best windows:** **Dec–Apr (esp. Feb–Apr)** — calm, viz >30 m, spawning + sharks + mantas. Wet/windy Jun–Sep (Jul wettest).

### 8. Great Barrier Reef (Australia)
**Water temp** (Cairns/north, S. Hemisphere seasons): Jul–Aug low ~23 °C → Jan–Feb high ~30 °C.
**Wetsuit:** 3 mm summer · 5 mm winter.
**Currents:** Inner reefs mild; outer/Ribbon Reef walls = drift dives timed to tides. Coral Sea/Osprey Reef significant currents (North Horn shark feed). **SS Yongala wreck = strong/severe current, dive at slack tide only.**
**Marine life:** **Dwarf minke whales Jun–Jul** (Ribbon Reefs — only licensed swim-with on Earth) · humpbacks Jun–Nov · **coral mass spawning November** (after full moon) · potato cod year-round (Cod Hole) · reef/hammerhead sharks (cooler months, Osprey) · green turtle nesting Nov–Feb (Raine Island).
**Best windows:** **Jun–Oct** dry season (Aug often best; Coral Sea viz 40–100 m). **Stinger season Nov–May** inshore (Lycra suits). Cyclone risk Nov–Apr.

### 9. Truk (Chuuk) Lagoon (Micronesia)
**Water temp:** 27–30 °C year-round, **near-uniform surface-to-depth (no real thermocline)** — 28–29 °C even at 60 m. Feb slightly coolest, Nov among warmest.
**Wetsuit:** 3 mm (full suit for abrasion protection on wrecks; 5 mm for deep repeats).
**Currents:** Sheltered lagoon — mild to negligible; mild current only on some wrecks/passes. Far calmer than open ocean.
**Marine life:** The wrecks ARE the reef — coral/sponge-encrusted hulls, 266+ fish species, reef sharks (deeper wrecks), turtles, eagle/marble rays, occasional mantas, barracuda/trevally. Low seasonal variation (equatorial).
**Best windows:** **Dec–Apr** drier season — calmest seas, best viz (24–32 m). Wet May–Nov still divable (cheaper, choppier, viz 10–18 m). Diveable year-round.

### 10. Cocos Island (Costa Rica)
**Water temp:** Surface 24–29 °C; **below thermocline ~16–22 °C, rare plunges toward 6 °C**. Thermocline begins ~18–30 m.
**Wetsuit:** **5–7 mm + hood** (hammerheads sit in the cold layer).
**Currents:** Strong (1–4 kt) + surge — advanced only, liveaboard only (~36 h crossing). Reef-hook hanging at Bajo Alcyone; Dirty Rock more sheltered. Currents strengthen in wet season (Jun–Nov upwelling).
**Marine life:** **Scalloped hammerhead schools Jun–Oct (peak Aug–Oct, 100–500+)** · **whale sharks May–Nov (best Aug–Oct)** · mantas wet season · tiger sharks & mobula schools Dec–May · silky/Galápagos/whitetip sharks, dolphins year-round.
**Best windows:** **Aug–Oct** for hammerhead/whale-shark spectacle (rough crossings, low viz). **Dec–May** dry: best viz (>30 m), calmer, tiger sharks/mobulas.

### 11. Komodo National Park (Indonesia)
**Water temp:** **North/Central warm 27–30 °C year-round**; **South cold upwelling 19–25 °C** (peak cold Jul–Aug, as low as 20 °C). Thermoclines common in the south.
**Wetsuit:** 5 mm (hood/vest for southern sites).
**Currents:** Tidal, strongest at full/new moon — powerful drift dives. Castle Rock & Crystal Rock (reef-hook pinnacles), **Batu Bolong** (ferocious flank currents), **The Cauldron/"Shotgun"** (committed drift to manta station). Downcurrents at exposed pinnacles.
**Marine life:** **North/Central mantas Apr–Oct** (Karang Makassar) · **South mantas Nov–Mar, peak Jan–Feb** (Manta Alley) · **mola mola Aug–Oct** (southern cleaning stations) · hammerheads (south) late Nov–early Apr · reef sharks, turtles, rich macro (best Nov–Apr).
**Best windows:** **Apr–Jun** sweet spot (May = best single month) · **Jul–Sep** best northern viz + mola mola (cold south) · **Dec–Feb** southern manta peak + macro (lower viz). Currents tide-driven year-round.

## PART 3 — Quick Cross-Reference

**Warmest water for comfort divers:** Maldives, Raja Ampat, Truk, Sipadan, Palau (all 28–30 °C year-round).
**Coldest / most demanding:** Galápagos (to 16 °C west), Cocos (thermocline to 6 °C), Komodo south (to 20 °C) — thick wetsuits + experience.
**Best big-shark action (hammerheads/whale sharks):** Galápagos & Cocos (Aug–Oct), Maldives (Rasdhoo Dec–May; whale sharks Jul–Nov), Red Sea (Jun–Aug).
**Manta hotspots:** Maldives Hanifaru (Jul–Oct), Raja Ampat (Dec–Mar), Komodo (N Apr–Oct / S Nov–Mar), Palau German Channel (Dec–Apr), Seychelles (Oct–Nov).
**Unique once-a-year events:** GBR dwarf minke whales (Jun–Jul) & coral spawning (Nov); Palau spawning aggregations (Feb–Apr); Galápagos whale sharks (Aug–Sep).
**Wreck diving:** Truk Lagoon (year-round, best Dec–Apr); GBR SS Yongala (slack tide only).

*Compiled from multiple cross-checked dive-operator, liveaboard, marine-research and sea-temperature sources. Temperature figures are reliable seasonal ranges (±1 °C between sources); marine-life peaks vary year-to-year with plankton and lunar cycles.*
