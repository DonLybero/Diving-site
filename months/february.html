Best Scuba Diving in February

Where the water is at its best in February, region by region.

← JanuaryAll monthsMarch →

February is Palau's moment — reef-hook season at Blue Corner with grey reef sharks stacked in the current — while Thailand's Similans deliver their calmest seas and Richelieu Rock's whale-shark window opens. In the southern hemisphere the Poor Knights hit peak subtropical visibility, and Socorro keeps serving mantas and humpback song.

Asia

4 picks, all at peak season — Fuvahmulah leads.

Fuvahmulah — Maldives

Peak 28°C 35m viz Strong current

The tiger shark capital of the world, where a shallow harbour plateau puts you eye-to-eye with resident tigers while deep drop-offs deliver threshers, oceanic mantas and schooling hammerheads.

What to expect in February: Tiger sharks year-round; strong window for silvertips (sometimes in the hundreds), hammerhead schools and whale sharks.. Conditions: Dry northeast monsoon, settled surface and gin-clear water..

Full guide: Fuvahmulah →

Similan Islands — Thailand

Peak 28°C 32m viz Medium current

Granite boulders, mantas and whale sharks in the Andaman Sea, liveaboard season only

What to expect in February: Mantas, whale sharks, leopard sharks. Conditions: Calm, excellent visibility.

Full guide: Similan Islands →

Maldives

Peak 28°C 32m viz Medium current

Monsoon-driven channel drifts; Hanifaru manta aggregation Jul-Oct.

What to expect in February: Hammerheads Rasdhoo; Fuvahmulah tigers (peak). Conditions: Peak dry season; superb viz.

Full guide: Maldives →

Okinawa Islands — Japan

Peak 21°C 24m viz Strong current

Winter hammerhead schools at Yonaguni, summer mantas at Ishigaki

What to expect in February: Hammerhead schools peak. Conditions: Coolest, clear, strong current.

Full guide: Okinawa Islands →

Oceania

4 picks, all at peak season — Palau leads.

Palau

Peak 28°C 40m viz Strong current

Blue Corner drift & reef-hook diving; lunar spawning aggregations.

What to expect in February: Snapper & moorish idol spawning (full moon); mantas; grey reef sharks. Conditions: Coolest; calmest seas; viz 30-40m+.

Full guide: Palau →

Poor Knights Islands — New Zealand

Peak 22°C 28m viz Medium current

Marine-reserve arches and caves with subtropical fish schools

What to expect in February: Stingrays, mantas, kingfish, mola mola. Conditions: 25-30m viz, warmest, peak season.

Full guide: Poor Knights Islands →

Yap — Micronesia (FSM)

Peak 28°C 30m viz Medium current

Resident reef mantas queue at channel cleaning stations year-round, peaking in the Dec-Apr mating season, with dusk mandarinfish and shark-lined caverns on the side.

What to expect in February: Manta mating in full swing with regular multi-ray gatherings on the barrier reef.. Conditions: Dry season peak, calm and settled with excellent clarity..

Full guide: Yap →

Truk (Chuuk) Lagoon — Micronesia (FSM)

Peak 29°C 32m viz Low current

World's premier WWII wreck dive; 50+ ships in a calm lagoon.

What to expect in February: Wreck reefs; reef sharks (deep wrecks); turtles. Conditions: Drier; calm; coolest but ~28C; best viz.

Full guide: Truk (Chuuk) Lagoon →

Africa

Tofo carries the region this month.

Tofo — Mozambique

Peak 28°C 25m viz medium current

Year-round reef manta cleaning stations at Manta Reef and Giants Castle, whale sharks off the beach Oct-Mar, and migrating humpbacks Jun-Oct, all reached by RIB launches through the surf

What to expect in February: Whale sharks, mantas, leopard sharks. Conditions: Warm wet season, occasional rain, strong viz.

Full guide: Tofo →

Central America

Coiba carries the region this month.

Coiba — Panama

Peak 27°C 25m viz strong current

A Pacific UNESCO marine park where cool, nutrient-rich upwelling feeds schooling scalloped hammerheads (best March-July), resident whitetip reef sharks, seasonal whale sharks and mantas, plus walls of jacks and barracuda over coral-clad seamounts.

What to expect in February: Whale shark window continues, dense fish schools, turtles and mobulas over the seamounts. Conditions: Dry, calm and clear; strong currents on exposed pinnacles.

Full guide: Coiba →

North America

2 picks, all at peak season — Socorro Island leads.

Socorro Island — Mexico

Peak 24°C 24m viz Strong current

Liveaboard-only Revillagigedo with friendly giant oceanic mantas

What to expect in February: Mantas, humpbacks, dolphins. Conditions: Coolest, hammerheads, clear.

Full guide: Socorro Island →

Jupiter — USA

Peak 22°C 18m viz Strong current

Gulf Stream drift diving with winter great hammerheads and lemon shark aggregations, plus a late-summer goliath grouper spawn on the wrecks

What to expect in February: Lemon sharks, great hammerheads, bull sharks on shark dives. Conditions: Winter shark season, strong drift, cooler water.

Full guide: Jupiter →

Caribbean

3 picks, all at peak season — Bahamas leads.

Bahamas

Peak 24°C 18m viz Medium current

World's premier shark diving - hammerheads, tigers, and whitetips

What to expect in February: Hammerheads, tiger sharks, reef sharks. Conditions: Prime hammerhead season, cooler water.

Full guide: Bahamas →

Bay Islands — Honduras

Peak 27°C 18m viz Low current

Roatán, Utila & Guanaja - affordable Mesoamerican Reef walls plus whale sharks

What to expect in February: Whale sharks (Utila), turtles, seahorses. Conditions: Whale shark season starts, drying out.

Full guide: Bay Islands →

Utila — Honduras

Peak 27°C 18m viz Low current

Budget dive-cert hub with year-round whale shark chances

What to expect in February: Whale sharks possible, turtles. Conditions: Whale shark season begins, drying.
