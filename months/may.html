Best Scuba Diving in May

← AprilAll monthsJune →

May opens the whale-shark ledger at Ningaloo — swimming with the world's biggest fish right off the reef — while Tubbataha hits the heart of its ten-week season and Komodo's currents turn the channels into fish soup.

Socorro closes its season with late mantas, and the Bahamas stay dependable before storm-watch begins.

Oceania

Ningaloo Reef carries the region this month.

Ningaloo Reef — Australia

Peak 26°C 32m viz Low current

Australia's largest fringing reef hugs the Western Australian coast, its coral gardens starting a short swim off the beach. The diving runs mellow and animal-rich: manta rays circling the cleaning station at Gulliver's, reef sharks queuing over cabbage coral at Asho's Gap, green turtles napping in limestone swim-throughs. The Exmouth Navy Pier alone justifies the trip, its pylons crowded with wobbegongs and frogfish, and whale sharks gather along the reef from March to July.

What to expect in May: whale sharks cruising the drop-off while mantas barrel-roll and turtles graze the lagoon. Conditions: calm seas and excellent visibility.

Asia

4 picks, all at peak season — Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park leads.

Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park — Philippines

Peak 30°C 30m viz Strong current

Marooned in the middle of the Sulu Sea, Tubbataha's two atolls and Jessie Beazley Reef are reachable only by liveaboard during a short season peaking April to June. The reward is the Philippines at full strength: sheer walls draped in gorgonians, grey reef and whitetip sharks on nearly every dive, and mantas or hammerheads riding the flow at sites like Washing Machine. Strong oceanic currents make drift diving the norm, so this UNESCO park suits experienced divers.

What to expect in May: sharks crowding the drop-offs while mantas and whale sharks cruise the reef edges. Conditions: flat seas with visibility beyond thirty metres.

Komodo National Park — Indonesia

Peak 28°C 24m viz Strong current

Tides squeeze between the Pacific and Indian Oceans through Komodo's narrow straits, and the currents they generate power some of the most electric diving in Indonesia. Hook into the reef at Castle Rock or Crystal Rock as sharks and trevally hunt the flow, fire through the Shotgun's channel, then drift Makassar Reef's manta cleaning stations. The warm north and cold, nutrient-rich south fish differently by season, so liveaboards cover it best. Bring drift experience.

What to expect in May: mantas working the north and central sites while whale sharks pass and reef sharks patrol. Conditions: dry, clear and calm with fewer crowds.

Layang Layang — Malaysia

Peak 29°C 40m viz Strong current

Stranded in the South China Sea far off Borneo, Layang Layang is a tiny oceanic atoll whose walls fall away 2,000 m into open blue. Divers come in spring, when schooling scalloped hammerheads rise along sea-fan-draped drop-offs like Gorgonian Forest, joined by dogtooth tuna and passing mantas. Inside the lagoon the diving stays calm and easy; outside, current-swept walls reward experience. Everything runs from the atoll's single fly-in resort.

What to expect in May: hammerhead schooling continues while dogtooth tuna and barracuda hunt the walls. Conditions: excellent visibility, warm and settled seas.

Koh Tao — Thailand

Peak 30°C 25m viz Moderate current

Koh Tao is where the Gulf of Thailand earns its reputation as an easy, generous place to dive: warm, calm bays over shallow reef for new divers, and offshore granite pinnacles that punch far above their weight. Chumphon Pinnacle and Sail Rock stack barracuda, trevally and — in spring and early autumn — whale sharks over anemone-covered rock. Budget-friendly and boat rides are short; skip the November-December monsoon and it delivers year-round.

What to expect in May: whale sharks still passing through while big schools of batfish and barracuda circle the pinnacles. Conditions: warm and mostly calm with the first southwest-monsoon breezes late.

North America

2 picks, all at peak season — Socorro Island leads.

Socorro Island — Mexico

Peak 25°C 30m viz Strong current

Far out in the open Pacific south of Baja California, the volcanic Revillagigedo islands are reached only by liveaboard, and their giant oceanic mantas rank among the friendliest anywhere, banking within arm's reach at El Boiler's cleaning station. Roca Partida's lone spire stacks whitetips on its ledges while hammerheads, silvertips, and curious bottlenose dolphins patrol midwater. Humpback song fills winter dives. Surge and current across exposed pinnacles keep this advanced territory; the season runs November through May.

What to expect in May: mantas gliding the seamounts while hammerheads school in the blue. Conditions: warming water with visibility beyond thirty metres.

Maui & Kona — USA

Peak 25°C 30m viz Low current

Hawaii's twin dive hubs pack volcanic drama into calm leeward water. Off Kona, dive lights draw plankton, and feeding manta rays, to Garden Eel Cove nearly every night of the year, while Maui day boats run to Molokini, a sunken crescent crater with visibility pushing 45 m and a sheer back wall dropping past 100 m. Lava caverns off Lanai and turtle-thick shore entries round out a destination that serves new divers and wall-drift veterans alike.

What to expect in May: mantas feeding under the lights while turtles graze and dolphins cruise the reef. Conditions: calm with visibility around thirty metres.

Caribbean

6 picks, all at peak season — Bahamas leads.

Bahamas

Peak 27°C 32m viz Medium current

No destination puts big sharks in front of divers more reliably than the Bahamas. Baited dives on shallow sandbanks bring tiger and lemon sharks within arm's length, great hammerheads and oceanic whitetips show in winter, and reef sharks patrol nearly every wall. Between shark runs there are upright freighter wrecks, coral-rimmed blue holes and ripping channel drifts like Current Cut. Day boats and liveaboards cover it, with Nov-May the prime window.

What to expect in May: oceanic whitetips circling the boats off the drop while reef sharks and dolphins work inshore. Conditions: calm warm seas with excellent visibility.

Great Blue Hole — Belize

Peak 29°C 18m viz Low current

Belize's Great Blue Hole is a flooded ice-age cavern 300 m across and 125 m deep in the middle of Lighthouse Reef, its rim a near-perfect circle visible from the air. The dive itself is a short, deep descent past stalactites at 40 m with Caribbean reef sharks circling in the gloom, best treated as an advanced bounce. The real richness lies on the surrounding atoll walls, so day trips and liveaboards pair the hole with sites like Half Moon Caye Wall.

What to expect in May: reef sharks cruising the rim while whale sharks aggregate around the full moon. Conditions: warm and calm with whale shark aggregations offshore.

Cayman Islands — Cayman Islands (UK)

Peak 28°C 32m viz Low current

Three islands, one theme: walls that fall away into genuinely deep blue. Little Cayman's Bloody Bay Wall starts at 6 m and plunges past 300 m, Grand Cayman stacks caverns, pinnacles and the diver-friendly Kittiwake wreck off Seven Mile Beach, and Cayman Brac holds a Russian frigate with its guns still mounted. Conditions stay clear and mostly mild, so day boats handle nearly everything, and the famous stingray sandbar makes an easy interval between wall dives.

What to expect in May: turtles drifting the wall while reef sharks patrol below and eagle rays soar past. Conditions: warm calm water with excellent visibility.

Bay Islands — Honduras

Peak 29°C 30m viz Low current

Strung along the Mesoamerican Reef off Honduras, Roatan, Utila and Guanaja deliver some of the Caribbean's most affordable wall diving, with seasonal whale sharks as the headline pelagic. Guanaja adds volcanic canyons, a black-coral seamount and the intact Jado Trader wreck at 24-33 m, while the Cayos Cochinos marine reserve keeps pristine elkhorn shallows. Currents stay mostly mild, and shore entries, day boats and liveaboards all work here.

What to expect in May: turtles cruising the crests while eagle rays sail past and seahorses cling to the seagrass. Conditions: warm calm water with visibility to thirty metres.

Full guide: Bay Islands →

Bonaire — Caribbean (Netherlands)

Peak 28°C 32m viz Low current

Bonaire perfected drive-and-dive freedom: rent a truck, load tanks, and roll into any of the marked sites along the leeward west coast whenever you like. The fringing reef starts meters from shore and stays remarkably healthy, from the fish-thick house reefs off Kralendijk to the sponge-wrapped pilings of Salt Pier and the shore-accessible Hilma Hooker wreck at 30 m. Calm, easy conditions make it ideal for new divers and macro hunters chasing frogfish and seahorses.

What to expect in May: turtles working the reef crest while frogfish ambush from sponges and tarpon hang beneath jetties. Conditions: warm calm water with visibility at its best.

Full guide: Bonaire →

Aruba

Peak 27°C 22m viz Moderate current

Aruba sits at the Caribbean's southern edge, below the hurricane belt, where steady trade winds shape a dry, sunny island and a sheltered leeward coast that dives reliably all year. Wrecks are the story here: the vast Antilla, the torpedoed Pedernales, a coral-covered freighter and a pair of sunken airplanes, most in easy recreational depths. Warm water, short boat rides and genuine shore entries make it a natural pick for new wreck divers and mixed-ability groups.

What to expect in May: turtle nesting season opening with green and hawksbill encounters and barracuda schools offshore. Conditions: warm and dry with strengthening trade winds and flat leeward sites.

Africa

2 picks, all at peak season — Seychelles leads.

Seychelles

Peak 28°C 18m viz Medium current

Granite sets the Seychelles apart from anywhere else in the Indian Ocean: ancient boulders tumble off the beaches and continue underwater as sculpted reefs, swim-throughs and fish-wrapped pinnacles. Inner-island diving off Mahe and Praslin stays mild and beginner-friendly, with hawksbill turtles and reef sharks routine and whale sharks cruising through from August to November. The remote outer atolls, Aldabra among them, are liveaboard territory for divers chasing wilder, sharkier walls.

What to expect in May: whale sharks feeding off the Amirantes and moving toward the inner waters. Conditions: southeast trades starting with conditions still good.

Full guide: Seychelles →

Hurghada — Egypt

Peak 25°C 25m viz Moderate current

Hurghada is the Red Sea's workhorse dive hub, and it earns the traffic: warm water all year, 20-30m visibility, and day boats fanning out to the Giftun reefs, dolphin lagoons and the four-wreck graveyard at Abu Nuhas. Sheltered coral gardens make it a natural place to learn, while the Carnatic and Giannis D give wreck divers real substance. Winter brings wind and chop, so spring and autumn are the sweet spots.

What to expect in May: dolphins, grey reef and whitetip sharks patrolling Careless Reef, big jacks and barracuda. Conditions: flat seas, 25-26C water, excellent light; prime window for the wreck runs.
