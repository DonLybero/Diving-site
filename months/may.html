Best Scuba Diving in May

Where the water is at its best in May, region by region.

← AprilAll monthsJune →

May opens the whale-shark ledger at Ningaloo — swimming with the world's biggest fish right off the reef — while Tubbataha hits the heart of its ten-week season and Komodo's currents turn the channels into fish soup. Socorro closes its season with late mantas, and the Bahamas stay dependable before storm-watch begins.

Oceania

Ningaloo Reef carries the region this month.

Ningaloo Reef — Australia

Peak 26°C 32m viz Low current

Whale shark snorkeling on the reef plus year-round mantas and turtles

What to expect in May: Whale sharks, mantas, turtles. Conditions: Excellent viz, calm seas.

Full guide: Ningaloo Reef →

Asia

4 picks, 3 at peak — Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park leads.

Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park — Philippines

Peak 30°C 30m viz Strong current

UNESCO atoll with sharks, mantas and pristine walls, liveaboard-only short season

What to expect in May: Sharks, mantas, whale sharks. Conditions: Flat seas, 30m+ visibility.

Full guide: Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park →

Komodo National Park — Indonesia

Peak 28°C 24m viz Strong current

Strong tidal drifts; warm north vs cold-upwelling south; mantas year-round by zone.

What to expect in May: N/Central mantas; whale sharks; reef sharks. Conditions: Dry; SINGLE BEST MONTH; clear, calm, fewer crowds.

Full guide: Komodo National Park →

Layang Layang — Malaysia

Peak 29°C 40m viz Strong current

A remote oceanic atoll where walls plunge 2,000 m and springtime brings schooling scalloped hammerheads in gin-clear water.

What to expect in May: Hammerhead schooling continues, dogtooth tuna and barracuda. Conditions: Excellent visibility, warm and settled.

Full guide: Layang Layang →

Fuvahmulah — Maldives

Good 30°C 28m viz Strong current

The tiger shark capital of the world, where a shallow harbour plateau puts you eye-to-eye with resident tigers while deep drop-offs deliver threshers, oceanic mantas and schooling hammerheads.

What to expect in May: Tiger sharks daily; peak oceanic manta season as they gather to mate, clean and feed.. Conditions: Southwest monsoon setting in with the warmest water and increasingly variable surface..

Full guide: Fuvahmulah →

North America

2 picks, all at peak season — Socorro Island leads.

Socorro Island — Mexico

Peak 25°C 30m viz Strong current

Liveaboard-only Revillagigedo with friendly giant oceanic mantas

What to expect in May: Mantas, hammerheads. Conditions: Warming, 30m+ viz.

Full guide: Socorro Island →

Maui & Kona — USA

Peak 25°C 30m viz Low current

Kona manta-ray night dives year-round plus Molokini crater

What to expect in May: Mantas, turtles, dolphins. Conditions: Calm, 30m viz.

Full guide: Maui & Kona →

Caribbean

5 picks, all at peak season — Bahamas leads.

Bahamas

Peak 27°C 32m viz Medium current

World's premier shark diving - hammerheads, tigers, and whitetips

What to expect in May: Oceanic whitetips, reef sharks, dolphins. Conditions: Calm warm seas, excellent viz.

Full guide: Bahamas →

Great Blue Hole — Belize

Peak 29°C 18m viz Low current

Iconic deep blue sinkhole with stalactites and circling reef sharks

What to expect in May: Reef sharks; whale sharks (full moon). Conditions: Warm, calm, whale shark aggregations.

Full guide: Great Blue Hole →

Cayman Islands — Cayman Islands (UK)

Peak 28°C 32m viz Low current

Crystal walls, Stingray City, and Little Cayman's Bloody Bay Wall

What to expect in May: Turtles, reef sharks, eagle rays. Conditions: Warm calm water, excellent viz.

Full guide: Cayman Islands →

Bay Islands — Honduras

Peak 29°C 30m viz Low current

Roatán, Utila & Guanaja - affordable Mesoamerican Reef walls plus whale sharks

What to expect in May: Turtles, eagle rays, seahorses. Conditions: Warm calm water, viz to 30m.

Full guide: Bay Islands →

Bonaire — Caribbean (Netherlands)

Peak 28°C 32m viz Low current

World's best shore-diving with healthy fringing reefs, dive-when-you-want freedom

What to expect in May: Turtles, frogfish, tarpon. Conditions: Warm calm water, top viz.

Full guide: Bonaire →

Africa

2 picks, all at peak season — Red Sea (Egypt) leads.

Red Sea (Egypt) — Egypt

Peak 26°C 18m viz Medium current

Year-round reefs & wrecks; offshore shark reefs (Brothers/Daedalus/Elphinstone).

What to expect in May: Whale sharks building; hammerhead schooling begins (best May-Jul). Conditions: Warm, calm crossings.

Full guide: Red Sea (Egypt) →

Seychelles

Peak 28°C 18m viz Medium current

Granite-boulder reefs; whale shark season Aug-Nov; calm transition-month diving.

What to expect in May: Whale shark peak (Amirantes); moving to inner waters. Conditions: SE trades start; still good.

Full guide: Seychelles →

Central America

Cocos Island carries the region this month.

Cocos Island — Costa Rica

Good 28°C 18m viz Strong current

Hammerhead-school mecca; remote 36h crossing, strong currents/thermoclines.

What to expect in May: Mobula schools tail; whale sharks (plankton rising). Conditions: Dry/transition; viz still good.
