Best Scuba Diving in June

Where the water is at its best in June, region by region.

← MayAll monthsJuly →

June belongs to the Coral Sea and the passes of French Polynesia. The Great Barrier Reef opens dwarf-minke-whale season on the Ribbon Reefs, Fakarava's groupers gather for the year's great spawning, Cocos starts building toward hammerhead high season, and the northern Red Sea settles into warm, glassy wreck-diving weather.

Oceania

5 picks, all at peak season — Great Barrier Reef leads.

Great Barrier Reef — Australia

Peak 24°C 24m viz Medium current

World's largest reef; dwarf minke whales Jun-Jul; coral spawning Nov.

What to expect in June: DWARF MINKE WHALES (Ribbon Reefs); humpbacks; mantas. Conditions: Dry; calm, clear; choppier crossings.

Peak 27°C 35m viz Strong current

Atoll passes with grey-reef shark walls and big-fish drifts

What to expect in June: Grouper spawn, shark wall builds. Conditions: Dry, excellent 30-40m viz.

South America

2 picks, all at peak season — Malpelo Island leads.

Malpelo Island — Colombia

Peak 26°C 25m viz Strong current

Walls of 200-plus schooling scalloped hammerheads, silky shark hordes and the rare smalltooth sand tiger on a remote volcanic seamount 500km offshore.

What to expect in June: Hammerheads, big silky shark schools and first whale sharks converge. Conditions: Wet season onset, warm water, deep thermoclines, good viz.

Cocos Island — Costa Rica

Peak 26°C 18m viz Strong current

Hammerhead-school mecca; remote 36h crossing, strong currents/thermoclines.

What to expect in June: Hammerheads building; whale sharks; mantas. Conditions: Wet begins; currents strengthen; viz dropping.

Full guide: Cocos Island →

Bat Islands — Costa Rica

Peak 29°C 15m viz strong current

Costa Rica's premier bull shark dive: at the Big Scare (El Gran Susto) a dozen or more 2.5-3.5m bull sharks cruise volcanic pinnacles at 20-30m, joined by giant Pacific mantas, squadrons of eagle and cow-nose rays and dense schools of jacks and snapper. A committing offshore day-boat trip from the Guanacaste coast, best in the warm green season.

What to expect in June: Reliable bull shark aggregations plus giant Pacific mantas, rays and baitballs of jacks and snapper.. Conditions: Warm green-season water with strong currents at the pinnacles; visibility swings dive to dive..

Full guide: Bat Islands →

Africa

2 picks, all at peak season — Protea Banks leads.

Protea Banks — South Africa

Peak 21°C 25m viz Strong current

South Africa's shark-diving capital: a deep 27-40 m reef of caves, pinnacles and gullies where Zambezi (bull) sharks patrol year-round, wintering ragged-tooth sharks pack the caves, and summer brings tiger sharks and schools of scalloped hammerheads. The sardine run passes offshore in June-July. Strictly for experienced, current-comfortable divers.

What to expect in June: Ragged-tooth sharks pack the Northern caves in mating numbers; bull sharks at their most consistent; the sardine run passes offshore drawing predators.. Conditions: Clear, dry winter conditions and the best visibility of the year; cold thermoclines, strong drift..

Full guide: Protea Banks →
