Best Scuba Diving in August

Where the water is at its best in August, region by region.

← JulyAll monthsSeptember →

August is big-animal month in the eastern Pacific: Cocos and Galápagos run their hammerhead and whale-shark peaks as cool, nutrient-rich water floods the archipelagos. The Great Barrier Reef and Komodo both sit at the top of their seasons — mola mola come shallow off Komodo's south coast — and the Azores deliver their best blue-water shark diving.

South America

3 picks, all at peak season — Malpelo Island leads.

Malpelo Island — Colombia

Peak 27°C 30m viz Strong current

Walls of 200-plus schooling scalloped hammerheads, silky shark hordes and the rare smalltooth sand tiger on a remote volcanic seamount 500km offshore.

What to expect in August: Whale sharks; massive silky shark aggregations; hammerheads. Conditions: Warmest water (~27C), calm seas, excellent blue-water viz.

Full guide: Malpelo Island →

Galapagos Islands — Ecuador

Peak 21°C 12m viz Strong current

Big-animal mecca; whale sharks & hammerhead schools at Darwin/Wolf.

What to expect in August: Whale shark + hammerhead PEAK (Darwin/Wolf); mola mola. Conditions: Coldest begins; cold thermoclines; rough; viz 5-20m.

Full guide: Galapagos Islands →

Fernando de Noronha — Brazil

Peak 26°C 30m viz Medium current

Huge spinner dolphin pods with sharks, turtles and 30m+ visibility

What to expect in August: Spinner dolphins, sharks, turtles. Conditions: Dry, excellent viz 30m+, calm.

Full guide: Fernando de Noronha →

Oceania

3 picks, all at peak season — Great Barrier Reef leads.

Great Barrier Reef — Australia

Peak 23°C 70m viz Medium current

World's largest reef; dwarf minke whales Jun-Jul; coral spawning Nov.

What to expect in August: Humpbacks; reef/hammerhead sharks (Osprey); potato cod. Conditions: Often BEST month: dry, calm, clear; Coral Sea viz 40-100m.

What to expect in August: Shark wall, humpback whales. Conditions: Dry, cool, clear.

Asia

2 picks, all at peak season — Komodo National Park leads.

Komodo National Park — Indonesia

Peak 27°C 30m viz Strong current

Strong tidal drifts; warm north vs cold-upwelling south; mantas year-round by zone.

What to expect in August: MOLA MOLA peak (south stations); N mantas; reef sharks. Conditions: Dry; N viz up to 30m; south cold/green; busiest.

Peak 29°C 18m viz Medium current

Monsoon-driven channel drifts; Hanifaru manta aggregation Jul-Oct.

What to expect in August: Hanifaru Bay manta+whale-shark PEAK (100+ mantas). Conditions: Best Hanifaru activity; viz 10-25m; full/new moon best.

Hammerhead-school mecca; remote 36h crossing, strong currents/thermoclines.

What to expect in August: HAMMERHEAD nirvana (100-500+); whale sharks; mantas. Conditions: Wet peak; thermocline (rare to 6C); viz 10-25m.

Europe

4 picks, all at peak season — Azores leads.

Azores — Portugal

Peak 24°C 30m viz Strong current

Atlantic pelagic diving - blue/mako sharks and mantas at seamounts

What to expect in August: Blue & mako sharks, mantas, dolphins. Conditions: 30m viz, warmest, calm seas.

Full guide: Azores →

Kaş — Turkey

Peak 28°C 35m viz mild to moderate current

The premier dive base on Turkey's Turquoise Coast, with 30-plus sites minutes from the harbour: dramatic walls and the Canyon, the intact sunken DC-3 Dakota, a full-size replica of the Bronze Age Uluburun wreck, amphora fields and swim-through caverns, all in famously clear warm-summer water.

What to expect in August: Rich marine life with frequent turtle sightings and pelagics on the reefs.. Conditions: Warmest water near 28C, thin 3mm suits, flat calm days and long clear visibility..

Full guide: Kaş →

Ustica — Italy

Peak 26°C 40m viz mild to moderate current

Italy's first Marine Protected Area (established 1986), where decades of protection have produced crystal water, walls draped in gorgonians and sponges, and unusually bold dusky groupers. Volcanic topography carves caves, arches and tunnels, headlined by the offshore basalt pinnacle Scoglio del Medico. Two underwater archaeological trails let divers hover over ancient anchors and amphorae in situ.

What to expect in August: Big groupers and pelagic amberjack and barracuda at their most active.. Conditions: Warmest water of the year and superb visibility; busiest month..

Full guide: Ustica →

Silfra Fissure — Iceland

Peak 4°C 100m viz Low current

Crystal 100m+ viz freshwater dive between tectonic plates

What to expect in August: Crystal viz, no marine life - geology dive. Conditions: 100m+ viz, peak season, easy access.

Africa

Protea Banks carries the region this month.

Protea Banks — South Africa

Peak 20°C 24m viz Strong current

South Africa's shark-diving capital: a deep 27-40 m reef of caves, pinnacles and gullies where Zambezi (bull) sharks patrol year-round, wintering ragged-tooth sharks pack the caves, and summer brings tiger sharks and schools of scalloped hammerheads. The sardine run passes offshore in June-July. Strictly for experienced, current-comfortable divers.

What to expect in August: Ragged-tooth sharks still in good numbers in the Northern caves; bull sharks throughout; guitarfish on the sand.. Conditions: Clear winter water; cold on the bottom, strong drift dives..
