Best Scuba Diving in December

Where the water is at its best in December, region by region.

← NovemberAll monthsJanuary →

December resets the calendar to the Maldives and Raja Ampat — manta season in full swing on both sides of the equator — while Socorro's winter giants return and the Bahamas run tiger and hammerhead action.

Cold-water divers get their moment too: Vancouver Island's plankton settles into its winter-clear best.

Asia

3 picks, all at peak season — Fuvahmulah leads.

Fuvahmulah — Maldives

Peak 28°C 33m viz Strong current

A lone oceanic island in the Maldives' far south, Fuvahmulah has no sheltering atoll, just deep water on every side and the most reliable tiger shark diving on Earth. Named resident tigers patrol the shallow harbor plateau at arm's length, while drifts along the drop-offs deliver thresher shark cleaning stations, schooling hammerheads and oceanic mantas. Day boats run everything, and the exposed blue-water diving favors confident, current-comfortable divers.

What to expect in December: tiger sharks holding year-round while thresher sharks crowd the cleaning stations and hammerheads school dependably. Conditions: northeast monsoon dry season returning with calm seas and clear water.

Phuket — Thailand

Peak 28°C 25m viz Moderate current

Phuket is Thailand's day-trip diving workhorse: big comfortable boats leave Chalong every morning for the granite boulders of the Racha Islands, the leopard-shark pinnacles of Shark Point and Anemone Reef, the King Cruiser wreck, and Phi Phi's karst walls. Warm, easy water and sheltered training bays suit new divers, while the wreck, currents and Racha Noi's manta-patrolled south tip give experienced divers real teeth. November to April delivers the calm seas and best visibility.

What to expect in December: mantas cruising in while leopard and blacktip sharks, turtles and barracuda tornadoes swirl at the Bidas. Conditions: prime dry-season diving with calm crossings to every local site.

Raja Ampat — Indonesia

Peak 30°C 30m viz Strong current

Off West Papua's Bird's Head Peninsula, Raja Ampat's four big islands and hundreds of karst islets sit at the epicenter of global marine biodiversity; Cape Kri holds the record fish count for a single dive. Nutrient-rich currents pump through the Dampier Strait, feeding seamounts like Blue Magic where oceanic mantas circle above carpeted wobbegongs. Reef hooks earn the fishiest sites, but sheltered cleaning stations and jetty dives balance the itinerary. Dive it by liveaboard or resort, October through April.

What to expect in December: mantas piling into the cleaning stations while current-swept ridges come alive. Conditions: visibility past twenty metres with a secondary rain spike.

Africa

3 picks, all at peak season — Tofo leads.

Tofo — Mozambique

Peak 27°C 25m viz medium current

A laid-back beach town on Mozambique's Inhambane coast, Tofo built its name on megafauna. Reef mantas hold court year-round at deep cleaning stations like Manta Reef and Giants Castle, whale sharks cruise offshore from October to March, and humpbacks migrate past between June and October. Dives launch by RIB through the surf; shallow inshore reefs suit newer divers, but the signature manta sites are deep drifts for experienced hands.

What to expect in December: whale sharks feeding inshore while mantas circle the stations and leopard sharks rest on the sand. Conditions: warm calm wet season with twenty to thirty metre visibility.

Protea Banks — South Africa

Peak 25°C 22m viz Strong current

Protea Banks starts where most reefs end: a deep offshore reef of caves, pinnacles and gullies lying 27-40 m down off South Africa's KwaZulu-Natal coast, dived as fast blue-water drifts with negative entries and open-water ascents. Zambezi (bull) sharks patrol year-round, wintering ragged-tooth sharks pack the caves, and summer adds tiger sharks and schooling hammerheads, with the sardine run streaming past in June and July. This is uncompromising, experienced-divers-only territory.

What to expect in December: scalloped hammerheads schooling while bull and tiger sharks round out the Southern Pinnacles pelagic mix. Conditions: warm water, powerful drift, often excellent visibility barring plankton blooms.

Mauritius

Peak 27°C 30m viz Moderate current

Mauritius wraps a volcanic island in barrier reef, so most diving happens minutes offshore where the lagoon wall drops into blue water. The west coast around Flic en Flac delivers the light-shafted Cathedral cavern and macro-rich ridges, while the north stacks wrecks and shark walls around Coin de Mire islet. It suits divers who want variety — caverns, scuttled ships, coral gardens — without long crossings, though January-to-March cyclones can interrupt; October to December and April to May dive best.

What to expect in December: turtles, morays and pelagics circling the northern islets as summer abundance fills the reefs. Conditions: warm and mostly settled early, humidity and showers rising toward New Year.

Caribbean

Bahamas carries the region this month.

Bahamas

Peak 25°C 18m viz Medium current

No destination puts big sharks in front of divers more reliably than the Bahamas. Baited dives on shallow sandbanks bring tiger and lemon sharks within arm's length, great hammerheads and oceanic whitetips show in winter, and reef sharks patrol nearly every wall. Between shark runs there are upright freighter wrecks, coral-rimmed blue holes and ripping channel drifts like Current Cut. Day boats and liveaboards cover it, with Nov-May the prime window.

What to expect in December: tiger sharks prowling the sand flats while the first hammerheads arrive and reef sharks circle. Conditions: cooler water as the shark action builds.

Oceania

4 picks, 3 at peak — Yap leads.

Yap — Micronesia (FSM)

Peak 28°C 30m viz Medium current

Few places deliver mantas as reliably as Yap, a quiet Micronesian island where resident reef mantas queue at cleaning stations in the tidal channels year-round. Dives are timed to the tide as short drifts or hooked hangs at Mi'l and Goofnuw Channels, peaking during the December-to-April mating season. Between manta runs there are shark-lined caverns to thread and a dusk mandarinfish ritual on the rubble, all dived comfortably by day boat.

What to expect in December: mantas beginning to court as trains form in the Mi'l Channel. Conditions: dry season, calm and clear with light currents.

Palau

Good 28°C 30m viz Strong current

Tides drive everything in Palau. Water pours through this Micronesian archipelago's reef passes, and at Blue Corner divers hook into the reef lip while grey reef sharks parade past in the flow; German Channel trades speed for manta rays circling a cleaning station. Between drifts come the vertical shafts of Blue Holes, the air-filled chambers of Chandelier Cave and lunar spawning aggregations. Day boats and liveaboards both work, but the strong currents flatter experienced divers.

What to expect in December: mantas feeding through German Channel while spawning events unfold and whale sharks cruise past. Conditions: dry-season calm with visibility over thirty metres.

Poor Knights Islands — New Zealand

Peak 20°C 25m viz Medium current

Two volcanic islands off New Zealand's Northland coast, the Poor Knights sit inside a strict marine reserve washed by a warm subtropical current. The diving happens in arches, tunnels and sea caves - Rikoriko is the world's largest by volume - where stingrays hover mid-water, blue maomao school in the light beams, and kelp-fringed walls crawl with nudibranchs. Boat-only day trips run year-round, best January through April, with sites from sheltered arches to pinnacles dropping past 45m.

What to expect in December: stingrays piling into the gutters while kingfish schools and rays sweep the pinnacles. Conditions: summer beginning with warm water and twenty to thirty metre visibility.

Lord Howe Island — Australia

Peak 23°C 24m viz Medium current

Six hundred kilometers off the Australian mainland, Lord Howe grows the world's southernmost coral reef, and the crossover shows underwater: tropical corals and temperate species share sites with fish found nowhere else, like the doubleheader wrasse. Sheltered lagoon holes give easy 8m dives, the volcanic Admiralty Islands stack up pinnacles, canyons and caves, and boat runs out to Ball's Pyramid - the world's tallest sea stack - bring Galapagos whalers and hunting kingfish. December through May is prime.

What to expect in December: sharks cruising the reef passes while kingfish and turtles fill the warming lagoon. Conditions: warm season with clear water.

North America

2 picks, all at peak season — Socorro Island leads.

Socorro Island — Mexico

Peak 26°C 24m viz Strong current

Far out in the open Pacific south of Baja California, the volcanic Revillagigedo islands are reached only by liveaboard, and their giant oceanic mantas rank among the friendliest anywhere, banking within arm's reach at El Boiler's cleaning station. Roca Partida's lone spire stacks whitetips on its ledges while hammerheads, silvertips, and curious bottlenose dolphins patrol midwater. Humpback song fills winter dives. Surge and current across exposed pinnacles keep this advanced territory; the season runs November through May.

What to expect in December: giant mantas circling the seamounts while dolphins play in the wake. Conditions: cooling and sharky with good visibility.

Vancouver Island — Canada

Peak 9°C 25m viz Strong current

Cold, current-fed Pacific water turns this British Columbia coastline into an invertebrate riot: Browning Wall is carpeted stem to stern in soft corals and anemones, wolf eels and giant Pacific octopus den in the rocks, and Steller sea lions buzz divers at Race Rocks. Big artificial reefs like HMCS Saskatchewan add wreck steel. Slack-window boat diving in a drysuit, at its clearest October through March, with Ogden Point offering an easy shore option.

What to expect in December: giant octopus at their boldest while wolf eels guard eggs and sea lions crash the dives. Conditions: cold water at its clearest, twenty to thirty metres of visibility.

Central America

Coiba carries the region this month.

Coiba — Panama

Peak 27°C 22m viz strong current

Panama's UNESCO-listed Coiba National Park protects a swath of eastern Pacific where cool, nutrient-rich upwelling turns seamounts into pelagic magnets: schooling scalloped hammerheads (strongest March through July), resident whitetip reef sharks, and seasonal whale sharks and mantas. Expect negative entries onto current-swept pinnacles like Wahoo Rock, thermoclines that can dip to 16-20C, and blue-water ascents. Most diving runs as day trips or multi-day expeditions out of Santa Catalina, and it firmly favors experienced divers.

What to expect in December: big schools returning in force while whitetips patrol and the first whale shark cruises in. Conditions: calming seas and clearing water with cool thermoclines at depth.

South America

Malpelo Island carries the region this month.

Malpelo Island — Colombia

Peak 25°C 18m viz Strong current

Five hundred kilometers off Colombia's Pacific coast, Malpelo is a bare volcanic rock rising from a seamount patrolled by sharks in staggering numbers. Walls of 200-plus scalloped hammerheads stack up over cleaning stations like La Nevera, silkies swarm by the hundred, and cold-season dives can turn up the rarely seen smalltooth sand tiger. Reached only by liveaboard, its surge-scoured pinnacles and powerful currents demand reef hooks and serious drift experience.

What to expect in December: largest scalloped hammerhead schools of the year swirling while Galapagos sharks patrol. Conditions: dry season with strong currents returning, cooling thermoclines and dropping visibility.
