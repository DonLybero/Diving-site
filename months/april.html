Best Scuba Diving in April

Where the water is at its best in April, region by region.

← MarchAll monthsMay →

April is transition month done right. Palau closes its peak with lunar spawning aggregations, Tubbataha's short liveaboard season opens in the Sulu Sea, Komodo warms into manta season and Malapascua's threshers keep showing. The Caribbean stays calm and clear — and Utila's whale-shark run begins.

Asia

5 picks, all at peak season — Layang Layang leads.

Layang Layang — Malaysia

Peak 29°C 40m viz Strong current

Stranded in the South China Sea far off Borneo, Layang Layang is a tiny oceanic atoll whose walls fall away 2,000 m into open blue. Divers come in spring, when schooling scalloped hammerheads rise along sea-fan-draped drop-offs like Gorgonian Forest, joined by dogtooth tuna and passing mantas. Inside the lagoon the diving stays calm and easy; outside, current-swept walls reward experience. Everything runs from the atoll's single fly-in resort.

What to expect in April: hammerhead aggregations building thickly while mantas and grey reef sharks work the walls. Conditions: calmest month with superb visibility.

Fuvahmulah — Maldives

Peak 29°C 33m viz Strong current

A lone oceanic island in the Maldives' far south, Fuvahmulah has no sheltering atoll, just deep water on every side and the most reliable tiger shark diving on Earth. Named resident tigers patrol the shallow harbor plateau at arm's length, while drifts along the drop-offs deliver thresher shark cleaning stations, schooling hammerheads and oceanic mantas. Day boats run everything, and the exposed blue-water diving favors confident, current-comfortable divers.

What to expect in April: tigers holding year-round while oceanic mantas build toward mating with whale sharks and lingering hammerheads. Conditions: tail of the dry northeast monsoon, calm before the seasonal change.

Malapascua Island — Philippines

Peak 29°C 32m viz Low current

Pre-dawn boats leave this small island off Cebu for one reason: pelagic thresher sharks rising from the deep to cleaning stations on sunken shoals, a near-daily encounter found almost nowhere else. The threshers now gather at Kimud Shoal, while Monad - the original site - draws tiger sharks. Between dawn runs there is real variety: Gato Island's swim-through tunnel, the Dona Marilyn ferry wreck and sunset mandarinfish at Lighthouse. Early starts and deeper shoal dives favor comfortable, experienced divers.

What to expect in April: threshers on the dawn shift while mantas and devil rays glide over the shoal. Conditions: flat seas with excellent visibility.

Komodo National Park — Indonesia

Peak 28°C 18m viz Strong current

Tides squeeze between the Pacific and Indian Oceans through Komodo's narrow straits, and the currents they generate power some of the most electric diving in Indonesia. Hook into the reef at Castle Rock or Crystal Rock as sharks and trevally hunt the flow, fire through the Shotgun's channel, then drift Makassar Reef's manta cleaning stations. The warm north and cold, nutrient-rich south fish differently by season, so liveaboards cover it best. Bring drift experience.

What to expect in April: mantas turning active at the north and central sites while whale sharks and reef sharks cruise. Conditions: dry season starting with manageable currents.

Similan Islands — Thailand

Peak 30°C 18m viz Medium current

Granite defines the Similans: huge smooth boulders tumble from white beaches to well past 40m, forming the swim-throughs and canyons of Elephant Head Rock on the exposed west, with gentle coral bommies and sand on the east. Liveaboards work the chain during the short open season, pushing north to Koh Bon's manta cleaning station and Richelieu Rock, a soft-coral pinnacle with Thailand's best whale shark odds. February to April brings the calmest Andaman seas and the biggest animals.

What to expect in April: mantas sweeping the cleaning stations while whale sharks and leopard sharks share the sites. Conditions: warm and calm with top visibility.

Oceania

3 picks, all at peak season — Palau leads.

Palau

Peak 29°C 40m viz Strong current

Tides drive everything in Palau. Water pours through this Micronesian archipelago's reef passes, and at Blue Corner divers hook into the reef lip while grey reef sharks parade past in the flow; German Channel trades speed for manta rays circling a cleaning station. Between drifts come the vertical shafts of Blue Holes, the air-filled chambers of Chandelier Cave and lunar spawning aggregations. Day boats and liveaboards both work, but the strong currents flatter experienced divers.

What to expect in April: grey-reef sharks massing in the channels while bumphead spawning draws crowds and mantas join whale sharks. Conditions: calm and settled with thirty to forty metre visibility.

Ningaloo Reef — Australia

Peak 27°C 18m viz Low current

Australia's largest fringing reef hugs the Western Australian coast, its coral gardens starting a short swim off the beach. The diving runs mellow and animal-rich: manta rays circling the cleaning station at Gulliver's, reef sharks queuing over cabbage coral at Asho's Gap, green turtles napping in limestone swim-throughs. The Exmouth Navy Pier alone justifies the trip, its pylons crowded with wobbegongs and frogfish, and whale sharks gather along the reef from March to July.

What to expect in April: whale sharks feeding along the reef edge while mantas and turtles work the lagoon. Conditions: warm and calm with great visibility.

Yap — Micronesia (FSM)

Peak 29°C 28m viz Medium current

Few places deliver mantas as reliably as Yap, a quiet Micronesian island where resident reef mantas queue at cleaning stations in the tidal channels year-round. Dives are timed to the tide as short drifts or hooked hangs at Mi'l and Goofnuw Channels, peaking during the December-to-April mating season. Between manta runs there are shark-lined caverns to thread and a dusk mandarinfish ritual on the rubble, all dived comfortably by day boat.

What to expect in April: mantas closing out the mating stretch while the cleaning stations stay busy. Conditions: dry-to-wet transition with mostly calm seas and first passing showers.

Central America

Coiba carries the region this month.

Coiba — Panama

Peak 28°C 20m viz strong current

Panama's UNESCO-listed Coiba National Park protects a swath of eastern Pacific where cool, nutrient-rich upwelling turns seamounts into pelagic magnets: schooling scalloped hammerheads (strongest March through July), resident whitetip reef sharks, and seasonal whale sharks and mantas. Expect negative entries onto current-swept pinnacles like Wahoo Rock, thermoclines that can dip to 16-20C, and blue-water ascents. Most diving runs as day trips or multi-day expeditions out of Santa Catalina, and it firmly favors experienced divers.

What to expect in April: hammerheads schooling in their biggest numbers while whitetips, mantas and mobula rays work the pinnacles. Conditions: late dry-season cool nutrient-rich currents with variable visibility on thermoclines.

Caribbean

2 picks, all at peak season — Utila leads.

Utila — Honduras

Peak 28°C 32m viz Low current

Small, cheap and endlessly sociable, Utila has certified generations of divers on its calm Bay Islands reefs, and it backs the training-ground reputation with real substance. Whale sharks cruise the open water off the north side year-round, peaking Feb-Apr and Oct-Dec, while offshore seamounts like Black Hills swirl with jacks and barracuda. Day boats reach everything in minutes, from easy sponge-draped walls to the upright Halliburton wreck at 30 m.

What to expect in April: whale sharks feeding offshore while turtles glide the walls and seahorses hide inshore. Conditions: flat seas and top visibility in whale shark season.

Great Blue Hole — Belize

Peak 28°C 30m viz Low current

Belize's Great Blue Hole is a flooded ice-age cavern 300 m across and 125 m deep in the middle of Lighthouse Reef, its rim a near-perfect circle visible from the air. The dive itself is a short, deep descent past stalactites at 40 m with Caribbean reef sharks circling in the gloom, best treated as an advanced bounce. The real richness lies on the surrounding atoll walls, so day trips and liveaboards pair the hole with sites like Half Moon Caye Wall.

What to expect in April: reef sharks circling the sinkhole walls while whale sharks feed off Gladden Spit. Conditions: calm dry-season weather with visibility beyond thirty metres.

Africa

2 picks, all at peak season — Red Sea (Egypt) leads.

Red Sea (Egypt) — Egypt

Peak 24°C 18m viz Medium current

Few places pack this much diving into warm, clear, year-round water. Egypt's Red Sea runs from the Thistlegorm's cargo holds of trucks and motorcycles to the coral walls of Ras Mohammed, then out to the marine-park reefs of the Brothers, Daedalus, and Elphinstone, where oceanic whitetips and schooling hammerheads patrol strong drift. Dive it from shore, day boat, or liveaboard; sheltered bays suit beginners while the offshore reefs demand experience.

What to expect in April: mantas cruising the offshore reefs in Big Fish season while early whale sharks turn up. Conditions: warm and calm, an ideal shoulder window.

Seychelles

Peak 30°C 30m viz Medium current

Granite sets the Seychelles apart from anywhere else in the Indian Ocean: ancient boulders tumble off the beaches and continue underwater as sculpted reefs, swim-throughs and fish-wrapped pinnacles. Inner-island diving off Mahe and Praslin stays mild and beginner-friendly, with hawksbill turtles and reef sharks routine and whale sharks cruising through from August to November. The remote outer atolls, Aldabra among them, are liveaboard territory for divers chasing wilder, sharkier walls.

What to expect in April: whale sharks gathering off the Amirantes as the plankton builds. Conditions: transition with flat seas and visibility twenty-five to thirty-five metres.

North America

2 picks, all at peak season — Socorro Island leads.

Socorro Island — Mexico

Peak 24°C 32m viz Strong current

Far out in the open Pacific south of Baja California, the volcanic Revillagigedo islands are reached only by liveaboard, and their giant oceanic mantas rank among the friendliest anywhere, banking within arm's reach at El Boiler's cleaning station. Roca Partida's lone spire stacks whitetips on its ledges while hammerheads, silvertips, and curious bottlenose dolphins patrol midwater. Humpback song fills winter dives. Surge and current across exposed pinnacles keep this advanced territory; the season runs November through May.

What to expect in April: giant mantas hovering over the cleaning stations while dolphins race the boat. Conditions: warming water with excellent visibility.

Maui & Kona — USA

Peak 25°C 24m viz Low current

Hawaii's twin dive hubs pack volcanic drama into calm leeward water. Off Kona, dive lights draw plankton, and feeding manta rays, to Garden Eel Cove nearly every night of the year, while Maui day boats run to Molokini, a sunken crescent crater with visibility pushing 45 m and a sheer back wall dropping past 100 m. Lava caverns off Lanai and turtle-thick shore entries round out a destination that serves new divers and wall-drift veterans alike.

What to expect in April: mantas swooping through the night-dive lights while turtles graze the reef. Conditions: calm seas with clear water.
