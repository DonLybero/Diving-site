Best Scuba Diving in July

Where the water is at its best in July, region by region.

← JuneAll monthsAugust →

July is austral-winter diving at its best: Ningaloo stacks whale sharks, mantas and the first humpbacks, the Great Barrier Reef runs minke encounters and humpback highways, and Heron Island sits in thirty-metre visibility. In the Atlantic, the Azores' seamounts fill with mobulas and blue sharks, while Fiji's soft corals bloom in the trade-wind currents.

Africa

2 picks, all at peak season — Protea Banks leads.

Protea Banks — South Africa

Peak 20°C 25m viz Strong current

South Africa's shark-diving capital: a deep 27-40 m reef of caves, pinnacles and gullies where Zambezi (bull) sharks patrol year-round, wintering ragged-tooth sharks pack the caves, and summer brings tiger sharks and schools of scalloped hammerheads. The sardine run passes offshore in June-July. Strictly for experienced, current-comfortable divers.

What to expect in July: Peak of the ragged-tooth mating aggregation in the caves; bull sharks reliable; sardine run and its predator trains active offshore.. Conditions: Cold, clear winter water with excellent visibility; brisk current on the deep reef..

Sardine run bait balls plus raggie and baited tiger shark dives

What to expect in July: Sardine run, raggies, humpback whales. Conditions: Cold, action-packed, viz variable.

Oceania

5 picks, all at peak season — Ningaloo Reef leads.

Ningaloo Reef — Australia

Peak 23°C 32m viz Low current

Whale shark snorkeling on the reef plus year-round mantas and turtles

What to expect in July: Whale sharks, humpback whales. Conditions: Cool, clear, excellent viz.

Great Barrier Reef — Australia

Peak 23°C 32m viz Medium current

World's largest reef; dwarf minke whales Jun-Jul; coral spawning Nov.

What to expect in July: Dwarf minke whales (peak); humpbacks; potato cod. Conditions: Mid-winter; coldest; best viz; trade winds.

Heron Island — Australia

Peak 22°C 32m viz Low current

Coral cay with shore diving, manta cleaning stations and turtle nesting

What to expect in July: Peak manta season; humpback whales. Conditions: Coolest water, excellent viz.

South America

2 picks, all at peak season — Malpelo Island leads.

Malpelo Island — Colombia

Peak 27°C 25m viz Strong current

Walls of 200-plus schooling scalloped hammerheads, silky shark hordes and the rare smalltooth sand tiger on a remote volcanic seamount 500km offshore.

What to expect in July: Whale sharks arriving; hundreds of silky sharks; hammerhead schools. Conditions: Warm, calm seas, occasional rain, strong viz, best all-round window.

Galapagos Islands — Ecuador

Peak 22°C 10m viz Strong current

Big-animal mecca; whale sharks & hammerhead schools at Darwin/Wolf.

What to expect in July: Whale sharks; big hammerhead schools. Conditions: Cooling; rough crossings; greener water.

Atlantic pelagic diving - blue/mako sharks and mantas at seamounts

What to expect in July: Blue sharks, mobulas, mola mola. Conditions: 25-30m viz, calm, peak season.

Asia

4 picks, all at peak season — Nusa Penida leads.

Nusa Penida — Indonesia

Peak 25°C 25m viz Strong current

Oceanic sunfish on cold winter upwellings and reef mantas cruising the cleaning stations all year, wrapped in some of Bali's wildest drift diving.

What to expect in July: Mola-mola season opens at Crystal Bay; mantas steady at Manta Point. Conditions: Dry season; cold upwellings, punchy currents, excellent visibility.

Maldives

Peak 29°C 10m viz Medium current

Monsoon-driven channel drifts; Hanifaru manta aggregation Jul-Oct.

What to expect in July: Whale shark peak (S Ari); Hanifaru manta aggregation begins. Conditions: Plankton-rich; lower viz; stronger currents.

Komodo National Park — Indonesia

Peak 27°C 18m viz Strong current

Strong tidal drifts; warm north vs cold-upwelling south; mantas year-round by zone.

What to expect in July: N mantas; reef sharks; mola season nears. Conditions: Dry; best N viz; peak cold upwelling south; busy.

Cenderawasih Bay — Indonesia

Peak 27°C 28m viz Low current

The only place on Earth to scuba year-round with resident whale sharks feeding under fishermen's bagan platforms, plus coral-clad WWII wrecks in glassy, currentless water.

What to expect in July: Multiple whale sharks feeding under the platforms; thriving coral-encrusted wrecks. Conditions: Peak season; glassy water, minimal current, top visibility.
