Best Scuba Diving in November

Where the water is at its best in November, region by region.

← OctoberAll monthsDecember →

November puts the Indian Ocean first: the Seychelles' second calm season brings glassy water and whale-shark passes, while the southern Red Sea keeps the shark encounters coming. La Paz opens whale-shark season in the Sea of Cortez, Tiger Beach hits its stride in the Bahamas, and Fernando de Noronha's dry-season visibility tops thirty metres.

Africa

5 picks, all at peak season — Seychelles leads.

Seychelles

Peak 28°C 32m viz Medium current

Granite sets the Seychelles apart from anywhere else in the Indian Ocean: ancient boulders tumble off the beaches and continue underwater as sculpted reefs, swim-throughs and fish-wrapped pinnacles. Inner-island diving off Mahe and Praslin stays mild and beginner-friendly, with hawksbill turtles and reef sharks routine and whale sharks cruising through from August to November. The remote outer atolls, Aldabra among them, are liveaboard territory for divers chasing wilder, sharkier walls.

What to expect in November: whale sharks still possible though rarer while mantas gather at the cleaning stations. Conditions: calm and clear with excellent all-round diving.

Tofo — Mozambique

Peak 26°C 22m viz medium current

A laid-back beach town on Mozambique's Inhambane coast, Tofo built its name on megafauna. Reef mantas hold court year-round at deep cleaning stations like Manta Reef and Giants Castle, whale sharks cruise offshore from October to March, and humpbacks migrate past between June and October. Dives launch by RIB through the surf; shallow inshore reefs suit newer divers, but the signature manta sites are deep drifts for experienced hands.

What to expect in November: whale sharks cruising the plankton lines while mantas and reef sharks fill the deeper sites. Conditions: warm early wet season with strong visibility.

Red Sea (Egypt) — Egypt

Peak 25°C 18m viz Medium current

Few places pack this much diving into warm, clear, year-round water. Egypt's Red Sea runs from the Thistlegorm's cargo holds of trucks and motorcycles to the coral walls of Ras Mohammed, then out to the marine-park reefs of the Brothers, Daedalus, and Elphinstone, where oceanic whitetips and schooling hammerheads patrol strong drift. Dive it from shore, day boat, or liveaboard; sheltered bays suit beginners while the offshore reefs demand experience.

What to expect in November: oceanic whitetip and thresher sharks thick around the Brothers while mantas cruise the reef walls. Conditions: cooling water through the prime shark month.

Protea Banks — South Africa

Peak 24°C 22m viz Strong current

Protea Banks starts where most reefs end: a deep offshore reef of caves, pinnacles and gullies lying 27-40 m down off South Africa's KwaZulu-Natal coast, dived as fast blue-water drifts with negative entries and open-water ascents. Zambezi (bull) sharks patrol year-round, wintering ragged-tooth sharks pack the caves, and summer adds tiger sharks and schooling hammerheads, with the sardine run streaming past in June and July. This is uncompromising, experienced-divers-only territory.

What to expect in November: huge schools of scalloped hammerheads sweeping past while bull and tiger sharks work the Southern Pinnacles. Conditions: returning warm summer water with strong drift and usually good visibility.

Mauritius

Peak 26°C 30m viz Moderate current

Mauritius wraps a volcanic island in barrier reef, so most diving happens minutes offshore where the lagoon wall drops into blue water. The west coast around Flic en Flac delivers the light-shafted Cathedral cavern and macro-rich ridges, while the north stacks wrecks and shark walls around Coin de Mire islet. It suits divers who want variety — caverns, scuttled ships, coral gardens — without long crossings, though January-to-March cyclones can interrupt; October to December and April to May dive best.

What to expect in November: reef sharks, barracuda and rays working thick reef-fish aggregations across the sites. Conditions: warm calm clear water before the wet season, the year's best conditions.

North America

2 picks, all at peak season — Sea of Cortez leads.

Sea of Cortez — Mexico

Peak 25°C 18m viz Medium current

Squeezed between Baja California and mainland Mexico, the Sea of Cortez trades coral walls for sheer biomass: sea lion pups that mouth your fins at Los Islotes, tornadoes of jacks over Cabo Pulmo's hard-coral fingers, and mobula rays massing in the blue. Day boats from La Paz add wrecks like the Salvatierra, and whale sharks gather in the bay from October to April. Advanced divers push out to El Bajo seamount for hammerheads; warm, clear late summer is prime.

What to expect in November: sea lions frolicking at the rocks while whale sharks return and mobulas gather again. Conditions: warm with great visibility as whale sharks return.

Full guide: Sea of Cortez →

Vancouver Island — Canada

Peak 10°C 25m viz Strong current

Cold, current-fed Pacific water turns this British Columbia coastline into an invertebrate riot: Browning Wall is carpeted stem to stern in soft corals and anemones, wolf eels and giant Pacific octopus den in the rocks, and Steller sea lions buzz divers at Race Rocks. Big artificial reefs like HMCS Saskatchewan add wreck steel. Slack-window boat diving in a drysuit, at its clearest October through March, with Ogden Point offering an easy shore option.

What to expect in November: sea lions mobbing divers at the haul-outs while wolf eels and octopus fill the dens. Conditions: cold water with superb clarity, twenty to thirty metres.

Caribbean

Bahamas carries the region this month.

Bahamas

Peak 26°C 24m viz Medium current

No destination puts big sharks in front of divers more reliably than the Bahamas. Baited dives on shallow sandbanks bring tiger and lemon sharks within arm's length, great hammerheads and oceanic whitetips show in winter, and reef sharks patrol nearly every wall. Between shark runs there are upright freighter wrecks, coral-rimmed blue holes and ripping channel drifts like Current Cut. Day boats and liveaboards cover it, with Nov-May the prime window.

What to expect in November: tiger sharks parading over the white sand of Tiger Beach while reef sharks circle overhead. Conditions: cooling water and good visibility with tigers reliable.

South America

Fernando de Noronha carries the region this month.

Fernando de Noronha — Brazil

Peak 27°C 32m viz Medium current

Some 350 km off Brazil's northeast coast, this volcanic archipelago sits inside a strictly protected marine park where visibility routinely tops 30 m. Day boats work canyons, tunnels and arches like Pedras Secas while huge spinner dolphin pods, turtles and lemon and reef sharks patrol the blue. Most sites suit newer divers in the dry Aug-Dec season, and technical divers get a genuine prize: an intact navy corvette upright at 62 m.

What to expect in November: spinner dolphins massing offshore while sharks sweep the points and turtles hold the reef. Conditions: dry, calm conditions with excellent visibility.

Middle East

Muscat & Daymaniyat Islands carries the region this month.

Muscat & Daymaniyat Islands — Oman

Peak 27°C 20m viz Low current

An hour's boat ride from Oman's capital, the Daymaniyat Islands are a strictly protected reserve where Gulf of Oman nutrients meet genuinely healthy hard coral. Winter brings clear water, leopard sharks and turtles; summer plankton blooms trade visibility for whale sharks that peak in September and October, with mobula squadrons either side. Add Fahal Island and the sheltered wreck-filled bays of Bandar Khayran, and it suits any diver who values animals over infinity-pool viz.

What to expect in November: mobula rays and eagle rays cruising while turtles crowd the thick reef life everywhere. Conditions: clear blue water with comfortable temperatures and reliably calm days.

Asia

3 picks, 2 at peak — Phuket leads.

Phuket — Thailand

Peak 29°C 20m viz Moderate current

Phuket is Thailand's day-trip diving workhorse: big comfortable boats leave Chalong every morning for the granite boulders of the Racha Islands, the leopard-shark pinnacles of Shark Point and Anemone Reef, the King Cruiser wreck, and Phi Phi's karst walls. Warm, easy water and sheltered training bays suit new divers, while the wreck, currents and Racha Noi's manta-patrolled south tip give experienced divers real teeth. November to April delivers the calm seas and best visibility.

What to expect in November: first mantas returning to Racha Noi with leopard sharks and thick soft-coral pinnacles in full colour. Conditions: dry season beginning with seas flattening quickly and visibility climbing.

Bali — Indonesia

Peak 29°C 30m viz Low current

Bali's mainland coast packs remarkable variety into short taxi rides: the coral-carpeted USAT Liberty wreck at Tulamben, black-sand muck slopes at Seraya and Amed, critter-rich bays at Padang Bai, and Menjangan's crystalline walls in the northwest. Warm, mostly gentle water and shore entries make it a superb place to build skills, while the macro life keeps seasoned photographers busy for weeks. Dry-season months bring the flattest seas and clearest water.

What to expect in November: nudibranch numbers surging as plankton returns while the wreck and Blue Lagoon stay fishy and photogenic. Conditions: exceptional clarity early in the month before the first rains.

Fuvahmulah — Maldives

Good 28°C 30m viz Strong current

A lone oceanic island in the Maldives' far south, Fuvahmulah has no sheltering atoll, just deep water on every side and the most reliable tiger shark diving on Earth. Named resident tigers patrol the shallow harbor plateau at arm's length, while drifts along the drop-offs deliver thresher shark cleaning stations, schooling hammerheads and oceanic mantas. Day boats run everything, and the exposed blue-water diving favors confident, current-comfortable divers.

What to expect in November: resident tigers daily while thresher season continues and hammerhead schools grow as the thermocline shallows. Conditions: early dry northeast monsoon with calmer surface and rising visibility.

Oceania

South West Rocks carries the region this month.

South West Rocks — Australia

Peak 22°C 24m viz Medium current

The drawcard at this New South Wales mid-north-coast town is a single rock with a 125m cave running clean through it. Divers enter Fish Rock's deep entrance, pass wobbegongs and bull rays in the dark, and exit the shallow end into gutters patrolled by critically endangered grey nurse sharks - sometimes twenty or thirty at once. Tidal currents and surge demand some experience, and grey nurse numbers peak May to December on straightforward day-boat runs.

What to expect in November: grey nurse sharks holding in the gutters while turtles and rays return. Conditions: warming water with good visibility.

Europe

Tenerife carries the region this month.

Tenerife — Spain

Peak 23°C 22m viz Low current

Tenerife turns volcanic geology into a year-round Atlantic playground: basalt columns, lava arches and yellow tuff terraces in water that never leaves the high teens. Green turtles hold court at El Puertito, stingrays mass off Las Galletas, and from December to March the sand hides critically endangered angel sharks — one of the few places on earth you can count on them. Easy shore entries and short boat rides suit new divers; wrecks and lava caves keep experienced hands busy.

What to expect in November: stingrays massing while turtles roam and the first angel sharks edge into the shallows. Conditions: still-warm water with quiet sites and clear autumn conditions.
