Waterproof W7 7mm Review

A semi-dry-grade 7mm loaded with seals, pockets and Kevlar knees for divers who want a suit that lasts.

← Back to scuba gear

#3 in Top Wetsuits in 2026

What makes it good?

Waterproof builds the W7 like a semi-dry: smoothskin flaps overlap on both sides of the back zipper, the wrists and ankles carry double seals, and owners consistently report almost no flushing. The 7 mm microcell limestone neoprene stretches four ways and arrives pre-bent at the elbows and knees, so the suit fights you less at depth than its thickness implies. It is also the only suit in this group with real working features: two flexible thigh pockets hold an SMB and a spare mask, and two anti-slip anchors keep a wrist computer's strap from migrating as the neoprene compresses. Wear points get heat-moulded aramid knee pads set in Duratex and a Duratex seat panel for ladders and tank benches. The men's range runs sixteen sizes including tall and widened cuts, but the chart runs small — a 175 cm, 75 kg diver reported needing an XL after the L refused to close comfortably. The zipper is a vertical back zip rather than a horizontal dry-zip, so it needs a buddy and stops short of true semi-dry sealing. At around $551 it lands between the Henderson and the BARE on price, and for cold, repetitive boat diving without a drysuit it is the most complete 7 mm here.

Where it wins

  • Semi-dry sealing on a wetsuit chassis: smoothskin flaps overlap both sides of the back zipper and the wrists and ankles get double seals, and owners report almost no flushing
  • The only suit in this class with two thigh pockets that actually swallow an SMB and a spare mask, plus two anti-slip anchors that stop a computer strap sliding on compressed neoprene
  • 3D-sculpted, pre-bent arms and legs in 4-way-stretch microcell limestone neoprene — soft and easy to don for a 7 mm
  • Wear points are armoured: aramid knee pads set in Duratex and a Duratex seat panel for boat ladders and tank benches

Where it falls short

  • The size chart runs small — a 175 cm, 75 kg (5'9", 165 lb) diver reported the L would not close comfortably and needed an XL; size up if between measurements
  • The double zipper seal is a vertical back zip, not a horizontal dry-zip, so it still needs a buddy to close and cannot quite match a true semi-dry
  • At around $551 it costs more than the Henderson while one owner still found it colder than expected in 20-21°C (69-70°F) water
