Beuchat Optima 2 Review & Best Price

Current second generation of Beuchat's warm-water workhorse, a 3 mm men's one-piece with a preformed anatomical cut and dope-dyed neoprene that cuts dye-process water use by 35 to 40 percent.

What makes it good?

The Optima 2 is Beuchat's mid-range warm-and-temperate suit, sold in 3 mm and 5 mm without hood alongside a hooded 7 mm. Beuchat splits the neoprene by function: Elaskin X.6.2 stretch through the body, with the noticeably softer X.6.4 under the arms so reaching for a valve or clipping a stage does not load the shoulders. The collar is zipperless with a glottis cutout, which sounds like catalogue language but simply means one fewer place for water to pump in at the neck. Wrists and ankles get smooth-skin seals — ankle zips and an Easy Wave wrist system handle donning — and the pre-shaped anatomical cut is designed to eliminate the water pockets that make a loose suit cold. Wear points are covered with Supratex 2 knees and PU prints on the seat and shoulders. The foam is limestone- and recycled-rubber-based, dyed with a Dope Dyed process Beuchat says cuts water use by 35-40%. Owner feedback in French dive shops centres on value: easy on, easy off, priced under €200 even in the hooded 7 mm. The catch for many readers is simply getting one — the dealer network is overwhelmingly European.

Where it wins

  • Two neoprene grades in one suit: Elaskin X.6.2 through the body and the softer X.6.4 under the arms, exactly where a stiff suit fights your finning and reaching
  • The zipperless collar with its glottis cutout removes a classic water-entry point at the neck
  • Smooth-skin neoprene at wrists and ankles seals against flush-through without needing tight cuff zips
  • Supratex 2 knees plus PU prints on seat and shoulders protect the panels that actually wear out on boat decks and under BCD straps

Where it falls short

  • Distribution is Europe-centric; North American shops rarely stock it, so sizing exchanges mean international shipping
