Mares Power Plana LT Review

The geometry of the classic tec rubber fin cast in a lighter compound: the short-blade frog-kick precision stays, while the weight drops enough to matter at airline check-in.

← Back to scuba gear

#10 in Top Fins in 2026

What makes it good?

The Power Plana LT takes the XR-Tech blade geometry of Mares' rubber tec fin and remolds it in TPV, dropping per-fin weight to 0.9-1.2 kg across the XS-XL run, with overall length between 51 and 56 cm. The result is slightly positive in the water, which suits wetsuit and travel divers and is precisely wrong for drysuit divers fighting floaty feet - they should stay with the rubber Power Plana. Test divers rate it smooth across flutter, frog and back kicks, with the stable blade profile holding a frog-kick glide without wobble. The blade runs directly into the foot pocket with no gap to snag a line, and the spring straps mount in multiple positions with a loop big enough for 5 mm gloves. The honest limit is output: the soft TPV works best at a relaxed cadence, and sprinting into current it folds earlier than the rubber version. At $217.95 MSRP it costs more than many all-rubber tec fins. As a travel-weight fin that still frog-kicks like a tec blade, it has few direct rivals.

Where it wins

  • 0.9-1.2 kg per fin across the XS-XL run makes it one of the lightest fins with a true tec-blade profile.
  • The stable XR-Tech blade holds frog kicks and back kicks without wobble; test divers rated every kick style smooth.
  • The blade molds directly into the foot pocket, leaving no gap or strap channel to snag a line.
  • Spring straps mount in multiple positions and the oversized loop works with 5 mm gloves.
  • Slight positive buoyancy keeps wetsuit divers' feet from dropping out of trim.

Where it falls short

  • The soft TPV works best at low cadence; pushing hard into current, the blade folds earlier than the rubber Power Plana.
  • $217.95 MSRP is more than many all-rubber tec fins.
  • Positive buoyancy is the wrong direction for drysuit divers already fighting floaty feet.
