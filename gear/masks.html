Top Masks in 2026

A mask is the cheapest piece of kit you'll buy and the one that can ruin a dive fastest. A poor skirt seal means a dive spent clearing water; a narrow lens means missing the eagle ray that glides past your shoulder. The ten masks below are the ones working divers and instructors keep choosing, year after year. They split into two camps: frameless designs that fold flat and give a huge field of view, and twin-lens frames that fit narrow faces and take prescription lenses. Every pick here uses tempered glass and 100% silicone skirts — no PVC anywhere.

What to look for
  • Fit test: press the mask to your face without the strap and inhale through your nose — it should hold on suction alone.
  • Black skirts cut glare and reflections for photographers; clear skirts feel less claustrophobic for new divers.
  • Low-volume masks clear with a single breath and make mask-equalising on descent almost automatic.
  • If you wear glasses, pick a twin-lens frame model that accepts prescription drop-in lenses.

1. Atomic Aquatics Venom Frameless

A premium frameless mask built around ultra-clear glass that removes the green tint of standard lenses, so colours at depth stay true — a real advantage on coral and for photographers. The dual-silicone skirt pairs an ultra-soft sealing edge with a firmer supporting base, holding its seal over smile lines and slim cheekbones alike. It folds flat into a BCD pocket, and the wide single lens keeps the view open and unobstructed.
