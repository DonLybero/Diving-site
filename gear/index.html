The buyer’s guides (articles)

  • Top Masks in 2026

    A mask is the cheapest piece of kit you'll buy and the one that can ruin a dive fastest. A poor skirt seal means a dive spent clearing water; a narrow lens means missing the eagle ray that glides past your shoulder

  • Top Fins in 2026

    Fins are where opinions get loudest — paddle versus split, short and stiff versus long and soft, rubber versus composite. Strip away the tribalism and the consensus across reviewers is remarkably stable: the same ten fins keep winning tests year after year, several of them decades-old designs that simply refuse to be beaten

  • Top Regulators in 2026

    A regulator is life-support, and the good news is that every model on this list breathes superbly — modern balanced regulators long ago passed the point where breathing effort separates them. What separates them now is cold-water capability, service network, travel weight and the feel of the breathe: some divers like a regulator that almost pushes air at you, others want firm, dry control in a current

  • Top BCDs in 2026

    The buoyancy compensator is the piece of kit you wear on every single dive, and the market has split three ways: traditional jackets that hug you reassuringly, back-inflate wings that trim you flat for photography, and minimalist travel rigs that weigh barely two kilos. The ten BCDs here are the ones instructors, divemasters and travelling divers actually wear

  • Top Dive Computers in 2026

    No category of dive gear has moved faster. Ten years ago a colour screen was exotic; today the mid-market standard is a phone-bright AMOLED display, Bluetooth log sync and a rechargeable battery, and the watch-style computers double as daily fitness trackers

  • Top Wetsuits in 2026

    Neoprene thickness tracks water temperature, but the ranges overlap more than any chart admits — how cold you run, how long your dives last and whether you add a hood all move the line. As a working guide: 2 mm and shorties above about 27°C, 3 mm from roughly 24 to 29°C, 5 mm through the mid-teens to low twenties, and 7 mm once you're in the low teens or below
