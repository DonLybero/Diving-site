Top Fins in 2026

Fins are where opinions get loudest — paddle versus split, short and stiff versus long and soft, rubber versus composite. Strip away the tribalism and the consensus across reviewers is remarkably stable: the same ten fins keep winning tests year after year, several of them decades-old designs that simply refuse to be beaten. Your choice comes down to diving style: strong-current reef diving rewards a stiff paddle you can frog-kick, travel diving rewards light weight and compact length, and cold-water boat diving rewards a spring-strap rubber workhorse that shrugs off a decade of abuse.

What to look for
  • Open-heel fins with boots are the default for most diving; full-foot only for warm-water boat dives.
  • Swap bungee or spring straps in for buckles — cheap upgrade, no more fiddling with gloved hands.
  • Stiffer blades transmit more power but tire untrained legs; efficiency matters more than raw stiffness.
  • Check the weight if you fly to dive — rubber classics can eat 4kg of your baggage allowance.

1. Mares Avanti Quattro+

Four longitudinal channels flex the blade into a scoop on every stroke, moving a large volume of water at a moderate kick rate — power that holds against current without shredding your calves. The long blade rewards a committed flutter kick and frog-kicks acceptably. Bungee straps come fitted, and the fin routinely survives years in rental fleets, which says everything about durability.

2. Scubapro Seawing Nova

A hinged joint behind the foot pocket lets the whole blade pivot like a propeller, so the wing sweeps water through the full stroke instead of only at the tip — quick acceleration with a cadence that is gentle on knees and ankles. Cast in one piece of tough Monprene, there is nothing to crack or delaminate. For divers who want paddle speed with a softer, joint-friendly kick.

3. Scubapro Jet Fin

Short, stiff, heavy and essentially unchanged for decades because the shape is right: vented rubber blades give precise frog kicks, back kicks and helicopter turns, which is why drysuit and doubles divers still default to them. The negative buoyancy balances drysuit feet. Sizing runs large — fit spring straps and the fins will outlast most of the rest of your kit.

A modern take on the rubber paddle: a wider, shorter blade for manoeuvring precision in confined or silty spaces, vents that smooth the recovery stroke, and oversized spring straps you can grab in 5mm gloves. Made in several compound stiffnesses to match how heavily you dive. The shape of choice for drysuit, wreck and cold-water divers.

5. TUSA HyFlex Switch

The blade detaches from the foot pocket in seconds, so a full-size fin packs to half length in a dive bag — a travel solution no folding gimmick matches. Assembled, it behaves like a conventional mid-stiffness paddle with a crisp snap at the end of each stroke, and the 20-degree blade angle keeps your kick in the propulsive zone.

7. Atomic Aquatics SplitFin

Water accelerating through the split generates lift like a propeller rather than push like a paddle, so a fast, shallow flutter kick becomes nearly effortless — genuine relief for knees, ankles and hips. The trade-off is real: frog and reverse kicks lose authority. For covering reef at an easy cruise, very little touches it.
