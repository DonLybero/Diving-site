Aqua Lung Reveal X2 Review & Best Price

Buckles mounted on rotating cardanic joints let the strap find the natural angle of the back of your head, spreading pressure evenly — no hot spots after an hour underwater.

#5 in Top Masks in 2026

What makes it good?

The Reveal X2 is the twin-lens half of Aqua Lung's Reveal pair, and its argument is the skirt: bands of differently textured silicone graded across the face, softer at the seal line and firmer where the skirt needs to hold shape. Because there is no rigid outer frame carrying the hardware, the buckles mount directly to the skirt and release with a single oversized button that still works in 5 mm gloves. The strap is a split design with a longer middle section and a silicone pad that spreads load across the back of the head instead of concentrating it in two thin bands. The two-lens layout accepts prescription lenses, which the single-lens X1 cannot. The trade-off of all that softness is setup: the skirt seals at a lower strap tension than most divers expect, and owners commonly report a dive or two of fine-tuning before it stops weeping. Aqua Lung also publishes no weight or internal-volume number, an odd omission for a mask sold partly on fit precision. It comes in about ten colorways with clear or black skirts, including Hi-Vis frames molded in yellow and orange.

Where it wins

  • The skirt is molded from bands of differently textured silicone instead of one uniform durometer, so the seal zone stays soft while the body of the skirt holds its shape
  • Buckles sit on the skirt, not a rigid frame, with a single-button release large enough to operate in 5 mm gloves
  • The split strap runs longer through the middle and carries a silicone pressure pad, which spreads load across the curve of the skull rather than two thin bands
  • Sold in roughly ten colorways with clear or black skirts, including Hi-Vis frames molded in yellow and orange for visibility underwater

Where it falls short

  • The very soft skirt is sensitive to strap tension; several owners report a leaky dive or two before finding the setting where it seals without over-tightening
  • Aqua Lung publishes no weight or internal-volume figure, so comparing it against low-volume rivals means guessing
