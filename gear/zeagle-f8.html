Zeagle F8 Review

Zeagle's top regulator pairs a dry-sealed balanced diaphragm first stage with a pneumatically balanced second stage. Sealed internals make it a natural pick for cold water and silty environments.

← Back to scuba gear

#14 in Top Regulators in 2026

What makes it good?

The F8 is the regulator Zeagle rebuilt from 20 accumulated design changes, and it shows most in the service ledger: a seat-saver orifice gives it a 2-year or 200-dive rebuild interval, and the second-stage service kits interchange with Atomic's, so any Atomic dealer can work on it. The balanced-diaphragm first stage uses a dry-sealed spring chamber to keep water and silt out, wears black chrome over brass, and carries 2 HP and 5 LP ports — four angled to the sides, the fifth aimed straight at the diver for sidemount or pony routing. The 227 g pneumatically balanced second stage has both a venturi switch and an inhalation-effort knob that work through 5 mm gloves. Long-term owners report unchanged breathing effort past 1,000 hours, and it is regularly compared to regulators several hundred dollars up the shelf. There is no swivel turret, so hose routing is fixed, and Zeagle's dealer network is thinner than the big brands', which matters when service is due. The warranty also still asks for an annual inspection even in the no-rebuild year.

Where it wins

  • Fifth LP port aims straight back at the diver for sidemount or pony-bottle routing; the other four angle hoses out to the sides
  • Second-stage service kits interchange with Atomic parts, so any Atomic dealer can rebuild it
  • Seat-saver orifice design earns a 2-year / 200-dive service interval instead of the usual annual rebuild
  • Venturi switch and inhalation-effort knob are both sized to operate through thick cold-water gloves
  • Owners past 1,000 hours of use report no change in breathing effort

Where it falls short

  • No swivel turret — all five LP ports are fixed, so hose routing is less flexible than turret first stages at this price
  • Fewer shops stock Zeagle than the mainstream brands; service can mean finding an Atomic dealer or shipping the reg
  • The warranty still requires an annual technician inspection even in the year you skip the full rebuild
