O'Neill Epic 4/3mm Review & Best Price

O'Neill's long-running 4/3 fills the awkward gap between a 3mm and a 5mm for divers who run cold. A 4mm core with 3mm limbs keeps warmth where it counts and stretch where you move.

What makes it good?

The Epic is O'Neill's mid-range surf suit, and divers keep buying it because it does the temperate-water job at roughly half the price of a dedicated dive suit. The 100% UltraFlex neoprene stretches more than most dive rubber, so the back-zip entry with pull leash works for wide shoulders, and donning takes seconds rather than a struggle. Warmth comes from FluidFlex Firewall thermal panels in the chest and back plus wind-blocking smoothskin, while the double Super Seal neck and glued-and-blindstitched seams keep flushing modest for a rear zipper. Rental operations use Epics because the rubber holds up to daily abuse, and Krypto Armor knee pads take kneeling on sand or swim steps. The trade-off is the same softness that makes it easy to put on: surf neoprene compresses more under pressure than dive neoprene, so below about 20 m it runs colder than the 4/3 rating implies. There is no seat or shoulder reinforcement for tank straps and ladders, and the warranty runs one year. As a travel and shoulder-season scuba suit for water above roughly 15°C (59°F) it is a sound buy; as a primary cold-water dive suit it is not.

Where it wins

  • At €239.95 list (US street prices similar) it costs roughly half of any dedicated dive suit in this group
  • 100% UltraFlex neoprene stretches more than dive-grade rubber, so the back-zip entry works even with wide shoulders or stiff joints
  • Rental fleets run the Epic because the rubber tolerates abuse; the covert blackout back zip and Krypto Armor knee pads survive boat decks
  • FluidFlex Firewall panels in the chest and back add insulation exactly where a rear zipper loses it, and the smoothskin exterior blocks wind on the surface interval

Where it falls short

  • It is cut and compounded for surfing: the soft neoprene compresses harder at depth than dive-grade rubber, so on a 25 m dive it insulates like a thinner suit than the 4/3 label suggests
  • No seat or shoulder abrasion panels — knee pads are the only reinforcement, so tank straps and boat ladders wear on bare jersey
  • One-year warranty is short next to Henderson's five years on materials and ten on the zipper
