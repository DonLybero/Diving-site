Beuchat VR 300 Review

Beuchat's current top-of-range regulator, machined and assembled in the Marseille factory and individually tuned on a hyperbaric test bench before it ships. Built specifically for temperate and cold water.

#11 in Top Regulators in 2026

What makes it good?

The VR 300 is Beuchat's top regulator, and unusually for the price bracket it is made entirely in the company's Marseille factory, with each unit adjusted individually on a hyperbaric test bench before shipping. The first stage is an over-balanced diaphragm design in bright chrome with Beuchat's cold-water system, so delivered pressure rises with depth instead of merely holding steady — useful insurance below 30 m in cold water. Port count is the standard serious layout: 2 HP and 4 MP, with a polyurethane MP hose to the second stage. That second stage is balanced, carries a venturi control, and wears a soft cover with a large dump zone for the exhaust. There is no inhalation-effort knob, so tuning beyond the venturi lever is a bench job. The DIN 300 bar version weighs 1020 g and the yoke 1173 g, which is heavier than travel-oriented rivals. Certification is EN 250:2014, and full sets run around 555 EUR in European shops. The catch for buyers outside Europe is logistics: Beuchat scuba gear has few North American dealers, so parts and annual service usually mean a European mail-order shop.

Where it wins

  • Over-balanced diaphragm first stage with Beuchat's own cold-water system, built for temperate and cold diving rather than adapted to it
  • Every unit is checked and adjusted individually on a hyperbaric test bench before it leaves the Marseille factory
  • The DIN version is rated to 300 bar and meets EN 250:2014
  • The second-stage soft cover has a large exhaust dump zone, which keeps exhaled bubbles out of the sightline

Where it falls short

  • Beuchat's scuba dealer and service network in North America is thin; stock and servicing mostly run through European shops
  • The second stage offers venturi control only, with no diver-adjustable cracking effort
  • The yoke version weighs 1173 g, 153 g more than the DIN
